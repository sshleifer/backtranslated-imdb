For beginners, I once met the director while he was going to the re-enactments of the Second World War with a vintage movie camera and making videos of events that looked like good DARNED. I really wish he had gone on like this. As much as I applaud what he achieved with a clearly "almost zero" budget, when I pulled out the DVD, I wondered why I spent almost two hours of nothing. There is no real plot to talk about, you don't really care what happens to the characters (perhaps Italian troops and some of the Germans), and the ending is yet another comment of "art movie" on the uselessness of war . I could have told someone how it would end once I passed the first ten minutes. I KNOW that the Germans would have some volunteer heroes to fight to the death, I knew that most of the Quartmaster soldiers would be killed, it was just too damn obvious! And while I'm on the subject, I was shocked to see that much of the Axis side was done well, yet the GI side did comically. All character development was clearly for non-English speaking roles. GIs have simply become difficult to use in this film. And I can't help wondering if they even had someone on the set who understood how the US military works? The sentences, the terminology, the actions were clearly written by someone who did not know the American army at all. If I had known that he had directed this film, I could have sworn it was directed and written by a German ...
