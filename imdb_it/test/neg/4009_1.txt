It has a bit of that indie queer edge that was fashionable in the 90s and that puts an explicit sell-by date on the visual style. The characters are uniformly apathetic and extremely impassive. Street hooligans in Greece wear new clothes without creases or spots. They all seem to visit the same marine hair stylist. They all show the same low IQ evenly when they do business. When things go wrong it is all because they are not true Greeks - they are lonely pastoral boys caught in a strange night world of the city. He complains greatly about disgruntled immigrants, but does not bother to investigate problems with Russian / Kazakh-Albanian cultures. If Giannaris had the right perspective on this project he could have made a wonderful Bel Ami production. The fleeting fleeting colors of beef for boys are the only spark in this generic programmer of small mobsters.
