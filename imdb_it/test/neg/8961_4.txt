A group of unpleasant inhabitants of the city descend on Le Touquet for a week's holiday. The stories intertwine, the characters fight, make friends, deceive themselves, have sex ... <br /> <br /> Blanc has assembled a stellar cast for his adaptation of Connolly's book, but with little success. What should be fun, on the other hand, is boring and irritating. All the characters are pathetic or unpleasant or both, and in the end, despite the farcical nature of things, this viewer didn't really care about what happens to any of them. <br /> <br /> We thank the always wonderful Rampling, as well as Bouquet and Viard but that's all. And Dutronc seems to be a little exaggerated with the handcuffs, if you ask me ...
