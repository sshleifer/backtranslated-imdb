Gregory Peck and Gig Young compete for the same girl and after Peck sends Young on a very dangerous mission, they blame him for his reasons. Feeling guilty, Peck takes on an almost impossible task of defending a fort, where they are numerically inferior to the Indians. Peck chooses soldiers for this mission he considers to be the scum of the earth and the actors who play these soldiers, Ward Bond, Lon Chaney Jr., Neville Brand among others, are excellent. The script is derived from a novel by Charles Marquis Warren who was a specialist in westerns, as a writer, director and producer. The idea of ​​using this type of men as heroes has inspired many films released later, including "The Dirty Dozen" of 1967.
