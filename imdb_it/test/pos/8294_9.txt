The only print of CHIKAMATUS MONOGATARI that I could find was abysmal - I almost couldn't see it. Which is a shame because this is one of Mizoguchi's greatest films. The story - which I believe has already been done in the past by other Japanese directors - is a little more decisive than my favorite Mizoguchi films (SANSHO THE BAILIFF and UGETSU MONOGATARI), and is essentially a story of tragic love story, in this is a transgressive novel that crosses rigid class boundaries. As always with Mizoguchi, there is an exquisitely expressed tone of challenge, and - bad press aside - I was very pleased. As with all Mizoguchi films, I look forward to the release of a restored DVD, whenever it could come ...
