In 1922, when it was announced that the Dismisal had to be done, there was a storm of controversy. This was an event that left open wounds in the hearts and minds of the Australian people. After some changes (listen to well-synchronized phones ringing to hide the names) the Dismissal went on the air. It was nothing short of brilliant. The cables were perfect. Max Phipps while Gough Whitlam is in the lead, followed by John Stanton as Malcolm Fraser and evergreen John Mellion as Sir John Kerr. Time has been created well, people's feelings have been well made and the political elements have not been transformed into two dimensions in melodrama as in so many American series. The Dismissal was a faithful re-creation of a period in Australia that some would prefer to forget and that we cannot forget. did not take sides and pointed out the errors and lies of both sides.
