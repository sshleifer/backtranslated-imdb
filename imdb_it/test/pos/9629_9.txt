The Patriot is a well-thought-out and well-made film that will directly attract the viewer into the story and keep them to the core. Of course, it's not in the same vein as Under Siege, et. al., but this is certainly the factory film of what could be described as the second era of Seagal films. Not much action films with a nod to a story thrown away for good measure, but a good story, combined with a point, and injected with some of Seagal's unique action sequences. Is this the kind of movie that hardcore action junkies will appreciate? Probably not. But for those of us who not only love our action but also appreciate a well-told story, this is a big step forward for Seagal. It's almost like the transformation that Clint Eastwood made while trying to get out of the shadow of his "Dirty Harry" series. Or man, if he had continued in the singular direction to which they were directed, he would have been forever tied to a specific role and guaranteed the brevity of their careers ...
