"Watch the Skies" (2005 - 60 minutes) is an excellent documentary on Sci-Fi films. It was produced and directed by the critic Richard Schickel, author of over 20 books on this topic. Mark Hamill is the narrator of the documentary. Schickel joins directors such as Steven Spielberg, James Cameron, George Lucas and Ridley Scott to take a journey through time and space to showcase some of the most memorable science fiction films of the fifties and some more recent classics. The documentary shows six different approaches: the paranoia of atomic warfare; The fantastic journeys on the Moon; The enigmatic planet Mars; Good and bad aliens; The post-apocalyptic world; and the future of humanity. Presents comments and scenes from the following classics: The Flying Saucers, Rocketship XM, Destination Moon, The Space Children, The Beast from 20,000 Fathoms, Godzilla: King of the Monsters, The Incredible Shrinking Man, Attack of the Killer Tomatoes, A journey to the moon , the angry red planet, the forbidden planet, the thing from another world, the Earth against flying saucers, I married a monster from outer space, the invasion of the body snatchers, the blob, the war of the worlds, the day The Earth Stood Still, ET: The Extra Terrestrial, The Omega Man, Planet of the Apes, The Terminator, 2001: A Space Odyssey, Things To Come and Metropolis. Highly recommended for Sci-Fi fans!
