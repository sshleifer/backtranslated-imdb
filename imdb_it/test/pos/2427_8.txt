What I find extraordinary in this terrifying film is that Altman, the crazy and savage boy he is, took the novel THAT COLD DAY IN THE PARK and the character of Sandy Dennis was originally a male in the book. He was a mentally isolated gay homosexual who looked out of his apartment window when he saw the bully. It is strange that Altman fans are not aware of how clever it was to change the gender of the main character; thus avoiding the homosexual taboos of gay life in the 1960s and ensuring that Dennis's solitary madness functions even better in the transposition. If you see the movie again, it will be clear how intelligent Altman's mind is ...
