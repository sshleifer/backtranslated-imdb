Hugh (Ed Harris) is a hotshot, a bachelor senator determined to run for president. One day, however, he happens to an old high school classmate, Aggie. Aggie (Diane Keaton) is an established and award-winning artist with a lovely face and an independent spirit. Hugh is impressed. He convinces Aggie to become her boyfriend. But will Aggie have to sacrifice her principles of honesty in the world of politics, where things are not always what they seem to be? And will he be able to withstand the rigors of a harsh media blitz? This is, for the most part, a beautiful love story for those who love stories of affection. Hugh and Aggie are absolutely in love and their jokes and conversations are a good sight. However, although the film tries to show political life in its reality, it fails completely. It does not matter. The production values ​​are high and the script is written very elegantly. With these advantages and the beautiful characters of Keaton and Harris, those who sit on the film will find good entertainment.
