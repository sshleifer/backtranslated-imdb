Trudy Lintz (Rene Russo) was a very lucky woman many years ago. She was the wife of a rich doctor and had a lot of extra-curricular money. His passion was animals and he dedicated himself to providing a shelter for the furry on his property. Trudy also raised two chimpanzees in her home to be more like children. They dressed in clothes and had many services. One day, he learns of an abandoned gorilla. Not knowing anything about the great apes, he relies on her husband's medical skills and expert advice to save the gorilla's life. Once out of danger, Trudy decides to also raise the gorilla as one of his sons. It works well for years and Buddy, the gorilla, is truly an extraordinarily intelligent addition to his home. But, Buddy is also a gorilla and his strength and curiosity become enormous. Will Trudy be able to keep Buddy in check? For those who love animals, Buddy is a must-have movie. Based on a true story, Trudy and her monkey develop a unique relationship in the annals of animal history and tradition. Of course, Buddy is not a real gorilla but a mechanic, in the film, but he is very close to looking completely real. Russo performs well as a lady ahead of his time and even supportive players are pretty nice. The costumes are exemplary, as befits the previous era of history, and the settings and production values ​​outstanding. But most importantly, the animals are here in abundance, not just Buddy, but the adorable chimpanzees, ducks, rabbits and so on. For those who want to watch a movie and be transported to the animal paradise here on earth, this is a great film choice. All animal lovers, and even those who just want to watch a great family movie, will go "monkey" on Buddy.
