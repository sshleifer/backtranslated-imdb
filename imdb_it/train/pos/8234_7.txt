"Fido" is to be commended for taking a tired, zombie-like genre and turning it into a more original cinematic experience. The atmosphere of the early 1950s is amazing, the acting terrifying and the entire production shows a lot of careful planning. Suddenly the viewer is immersed in a world of beautiful classic cars, "Eisenhower era" dress, art deco furniture and zombie servants. It would be very easy to dismiss "Fido" as a cartoon-like fuzz, similar to "Tank Girl", but the two films are very different. "Fido has a structure, a script that tells a story and a higher acting. Don't make mistakes, this is a brave black comedy that succeeds where so many others have failed.
