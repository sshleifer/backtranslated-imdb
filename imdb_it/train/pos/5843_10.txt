Lubitsch's latest production, but not his less interesting film. Somehow, largely ignored by critics, failing to finish himself and since the film was not co-signed by Preminger who did most of the staging ... A very strange mix of musicals (a recollection of The Merry Widow?) and the classic Lubitsch sentimentality (an impossible love story like Cluny Brown), but very intelligent and intelligent, but not intended as a nostalgia for a lost world, but rather a testament to eternal feelings that prevail over the madness of humanity and in particular of men in time of war with a "moral" lesson still true today as it was in 1948. Billy savage as a response to Preminger who grieved at Lubitsch's funeral about losing a great man replied that we still had his films and that he sums up everything about that Lady in Ermellino ...
