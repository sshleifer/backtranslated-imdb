It was really exciting. As a French expatriate, I particularly liked this TV movie, which I believe deserves a wider distribution. The acting was good, and the bonds between each member of this rural French family seemed so strong and so natural. Without doubt a great film on the concept of family and inclusion in front of homosexuality and AIDS. One of the most important aspects of the film is this privileged relationship between the older son (Leo), who has contracted HIV, and the younger son (Marcel), to whom the rest of the family tries to hide the situation. The two characters progressively explore each other while time runs out for Leo, who is not willing to spend the rest of his life surviving under the drug ...
