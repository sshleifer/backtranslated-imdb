On the outskirts of São Paulo, the infamous dysfunctional and hypocritical middle class family of Teodoro (Giulio Lopes), Cláudia (Leona Cavalli) and the adolescent Soninha (Sílvia Lourenço) have profound secrets. The religious Teodoro is really a hit man, hired to kill the people of the neighborhood with his friend Waldomiro (Ailton Graça). She has a lover, the very devoted woman Terezinha (Martha Meola), and wants to regenerate herself by going to the country with her. Cláudia has a young lover, Júlio (Ismael de Araújo), who delivers meat for her father's butcher. Soninha is a sixteen-year-old teenager from the suburbs, who has an active sex life, who smokes weed and loves heavy metal. When Júlio is killed and castrated in their neighborhood, the lives of family members change. <br /> <br /> "Contra Todos" is a low-budget Brazilian film that describes life on the outskirts of a large Brazilian city. The plot is very real, it uses the usual elements of the poor area of ​​the big Brazilian cities (drug dealers, assassins, evangelical religious fanatics, hopeless teenagers etc.), It has many points of the plot and a surprising end, and the characters have shows excellent, very natural acting and making the story totally believable. The camera follows the characters, giving a great dynamic to the film. In the DVD Extras, director Roberto Moreira explains that his script had no lines, only the description of the situations, and was only partially disclosed a week before the start of filming. Actors have training courses in laboratories and have used a lot of improvisation, being the reason for such a natural acting. My vote is eight. <br /> <br /> Title (Brazil): "Contra Todos" ("Against Everybody")
