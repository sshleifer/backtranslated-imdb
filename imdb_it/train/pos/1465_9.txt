His choice of films, the basic "presumption" of production (which places him in the sets or in the simulacra of the films he is commenting on) and his delivery are exceptional! But if you want Freud, know that you are taking Lacan's version of Zizek, which should not be confused with Lacan himself. As usual, Zizek offers complex ideas tastefully and convincingly. The problem is that it is also quite mercurial and therefore there may be more in its taste than in the real content. Kinematically, it is a gem. Psychologically, this will have people of all persuasions (Freudians, Lacanians, and Jungians) who scratch their heads but still look for popcorn. Zizek is a phenomenon and a pop icon for itself.
