There are four great films that describe the Vietnam War. They are (in no particular order: Apocalypse Now, Born on the Fourth of July, Platoon, and finally Tigerland. Everyone except Tigerland concentrates on the real war and the men inside. Tigerland focuses on men in advanced training for the war of "Vietnam. The character of Boz is one of the most important depictions of a man who questions the war, and the absurdity of it. This has been done in many war films, but rarely in the training camp. Furthermore, this is a very complex character, whose method with dealing with his feelings and emotions are the driving force of this film.The character of Boz makes this film so good. It is a shame not to have obtained a major release. It belongs to the shelf of any movie fan next to the previously mentioned movie titles.
