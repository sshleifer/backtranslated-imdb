Hotel Du Nord is a compelling drama of guilt in which Marcel Carne played a fun tale of unlucky love that also serves as a revolt against the cruel world. The film is based entirely on a pair of unfortunate lovers. Pierre and Renee were wrong when they believed that suicide would end their misery. The Hotel Du Nord has its own inimitable charm as its inhabitants have become an essential part of the establishment. There is an element of cohesion in which everyone flocks to the Hotel Du Nord to eat, chat, etc. Marcel Carne has remained faithful to the spirit of the films produced in the 1930s and 1940s, as the Hotel Du Nord has a certain kind of nostalgic atmosphere. nostalgia for black and white gives a unique kind of poetic realism to his work. The Hotel Du Nord can be defined as the quintessence of cinematic populism. The 14 July ball scene on the banks of the Saint Martin canal remains a magnificent sequence. the great popularity can be judged from the fact that Hotel Du Nord has been declared a national monument.
