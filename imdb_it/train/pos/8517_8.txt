Russ and Valerie are discussing starting a family. The couple lives in an elegant apartment and runs an auction company that deals with valuable collectibles. At the same time, a dedicated adoption agency owner takes a mini vacation and leaves the orphanage under the responsibility of his father (Leslie Nielsen). Father Harry is in the rental business and has the brilliant idea of ​​"renting" some of the children in the orphanage to couples like Russ and Valerie. Harry, who realizes the couple's dilemma, offers a family of siblings for a 10-day rental period! Brandon, Kyle and Molly enter the apartment with their temporary parents, with fun consequences, as the new assistants are inexperienced with the children. But where is the possibility of a happy ending? This is a dear family film. The actors, including Nielsen as the wheel salesman and Christopher Lloyd as the kind concierge of the apartment, are all wonderful. The script is lively and fun and the overall production values ​​are quite high. Yes, if only life could be like this! Orphaned children around the world deserve a chance to prove that they are lovable and can give so much joy to parents who are considering adoption. If you want to show a film to your family that has its good values ​​but is also a lot of fun, find this movie. It is guaranteed that everyone laughs, even if their hearts are melting.
