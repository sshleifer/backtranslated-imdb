I was expecting a documentary focusing on the North Carolina tobacco industry. Instead I saw a man insulting that his great-grandfather lost his tobacco empire in the Duke family. And this went on and on. If Mr. McElwee's family had prevailed over the Dukes, I doubt that Mr. McElwee would have any problems with the death toll caused by tobacco-related illnesses. I grew up near the area where Mr. McElwee's family started their tobacco business; I expected more of McEwee's constant attention to his family. I learned very little about the history of tobacco in the NC economy and about the ramifications of the state's economy through more stringent tobacco regulation. The innumerable references to the film "Bright Leaves" are out of place - What if Gary Cooper played Mr. McElwee's great-grandfather? Does the viewer gain any understanding of the role of tobacco in the North Carolina economy by showing the old footage of a fiction film? I did not
