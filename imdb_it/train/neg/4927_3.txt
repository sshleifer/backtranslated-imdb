Lois Weber, a self-proclaimed missionary through film, wrote, directed and produced other films on controversial topics, but this could be the first to gain a broad view, thanks to TCM. This film is her accusation of abortion, but she cleverly confuses the problem by introducing eugenics and birth control, leaving the impression that they are somehow equivalent to abortion. His talent in writing and other cinematographic skills are well displayed here, but one can be forgiven for wanting them to use them less educationally. If you wondered what Tyrone Power, Jr.'s "famous father" was, here's your chance. Even the fashions and cars of 1916 are on display to increase the interest of this museum piece. It's pleasant even if you don't appreciate propaganda.
