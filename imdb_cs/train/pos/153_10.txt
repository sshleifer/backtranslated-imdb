This film is simply a film masterpiece. The film is brilliantly moved by intense shots that move the sensitive viewer deeply. The film opens during the Spanish Civil War when a group of children seeks their revenge for another child. In fact, in their world, they are portraying what they have witnessed in the adult world around them. Later we will meet three of these children as adults in the sanatorium. Here we see what worked on each of them. One is an isolated sexually suppressed patient. Another man is a cheater who has become ill. The third child, a young lady, became a nun and served in the sanatorium. This film is an alegory of the effect of psyche violence. This film has a climax that is definitely not for squeamish members of the audience, but it is both logical and deeply moving. The actor is excellent and the script is quite well written. There is a musical score that provides under the strain of fear in this movie. It's not a movie for aspirants but a film for a thoughtful audience.
