It has a bit of that indie queer edge that was hip in the 90s and which puts an explicit sale-by date on the visual style. The characters are uniformly apathetic and farcically deadpan. Street hoodlums in Greece wear new clothes from a box without creases or stains. Everyone seems to visit the same sea hairdresser. All uniformly show the same low IQs when performing their rude trades underground. When things go wrong because they're not real Greeks - they are shepherd's sun boys caught in a strange night city world. He makes a big whining about dissatisfied immigrants, but he never bothered to really investigate problems with Russian / Kazakh / Albanian cultures. If Giannaris had the right perspective on this project, it could have meant a stunning Bel Ami production. The fleeting flashes of tinted boy-beef are the only spark in this generic little programmer.
