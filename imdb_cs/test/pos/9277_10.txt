"Watch the Sky" (2005 - 60 minutes) is an excellent documentary about science fiction films. Produced and directed by critic Richard Schickel, author of more than 20 books on the subject. Mark Hamill is a documentary narrator. Schickel joins directors such as Steven Spielberg, James Cameron, George Lucas and Ridley Scott who travel in time and space to show some of the most memorable science fiction films of the 1950s as well as some of the newer classics. The documentary shows six different approaches: Paranoia of Atomic War; Fantastic trips to the moon; The Mysterious Planet Mars; Good and evil aliens; Poapokalyptický svět; and the future of mankind. Featuring comments and scenes from the following classics: Flying Plates, Rocketship XM, Destination Moon, Space Kids, Beast of 20,000 Dreams, Godzilla: King of Monsters, Incredible Shrinking Man, Attack of Killer Tomatoes, Trip to the Moon, Angry Red Planet , Things from Another World, Earth vs. Flying Plates, I Taken a Monster from Space, Invasion of the Body Hijackers, Blob, War of the Worlds, Earth Day Stopped, ET: Extra Earth, Omega Man, Planet of Apes, Terminator, 2001: Space Odyssey, Things to Come and Metropolis. We highly recommend Science Fiction fans!
