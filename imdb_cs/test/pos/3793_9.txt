In response to the numbness of the real combat films at that time, the war public was increasingly turning to humor as an escape from monotony and anxiety? Charlie Chaplin was afraid that his big "Shoulder Arm" would offend people, but became his biggest hit? In it, Charlie, about happiness, courage and devilish ingenuity wins the war individually and brings the Kaiser captive to London in triumph? The main difference between this jolly burlesque and some serious war dramas was that everything turned out to be a dream for Charlie?
