"Lackawanna Blues" is so emotionally powerful in its depiction of urban blacks in the 1950s and early 1960s. A culture of joy and sorrow specific to working blacks that existed outside the mainstream culture. they lived in imperfect lives, but they maintained strong positive values ​​of love, loyalty, and honor, even though the characters occasionally moved away from these values, did not deny the meaning of values, instead recognized and accepted their own imperfections and other characters. Without judgment, passed through the life of a "nanny". The central characters were strong and believable, the settings realistic and bringing personal memories of the past era. The pre-integrative urban life was a time of sensory intoxication, sight, sound and smell that could be almost experienced by watching the drama "Lackawanna Blues" unfold.
