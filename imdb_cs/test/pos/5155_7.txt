There's an endless fascination with where writer-director Roy Andersson wants us in his fourth "You, The Living" section. With fifty or so some semi-related vignettes that are connected with the hobby of tragicomic hyper-reality, its whimsical interpretations and symbolic instances of life that weigh us all in this great cosmic Sisyphus struggle. The very simplicity of these medallions acts to dramatize the subtlety and immense rarity of being beyond the symbiotic relationships we have among us. Andersson could lessen the complexity of the human condition through harsh and fast cynicism more than he should, but it also reminds us of the natural, reassuring glory of awakening every morning to a new tomorrow when we are all aware of our own difference. forms of arrested development.
