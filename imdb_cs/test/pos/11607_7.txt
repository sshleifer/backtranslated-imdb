Chokher Bali? Passion Play. Based on the Rabindranath Tagore novel of the same name, it is a classic story of deception, adultery and exploitation of relationships. Director Rituparno Ghosh, which takes place in 1900, transformed the acclaimed literature of Nobel laureates into a kind visual comfort. Tagore's story deals in detail with Bengali society through its central character, a rebellious widow who wants to live her own life. We are taken to a picturesque part of Bengal where we meet our heroine, the beautiful, young widow Binodini (Aishwarya Rai). Despite his beautiful appearance, two handsome men, wealthy Mahindra (Prosenjit Chatterji) and his friend Behari (Toto Roychowdhury), denied marrying her. Mahindra chooses naive Ashalata (Raima Sen) through Binodin and marries her. Devoted to life in the country, the carefree Binodini accompanies Mahindra's mother to Calcutta as administrator. Soon, her friendship with Ashalata flourishes. It seems that the two who approach each other as "Chokher Bali" share a lasting bond. English speaking Binodin captures a special place in the house. But soon she will reveal her real face. Handling good-natured Ashlata, Binodini approaches Mahindra and fills her sexual desires. When Mahindra's angry mother throws her out, Binodini seeks comfort from Behari's reluctance. The rest of the story shows how the lives of these four characters cross and culminate in an unimaginable peak? Aishwarya goes through the role of a manipulative, rebellious lady, still gaining audience sympathy with the elegance of the ballet dancer.
