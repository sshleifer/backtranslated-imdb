What attracts man to military service? What prepares a man to survive brutal torture as a prisoner of war? What desperation leads to the planning and realization of the jungle leakage of Southeast Asia? How does he deal with the spirits and memories he returns home with? Herzog tries to answer these questions in his diary Dengler, a German emigrant and US Navy pilot shot down over Laos in 1966, who was captured, tortured and starved, but eventually escaped to pursue him for the rest of his life. . A strong and personal account firsthand about the person, his life and experiences, which are described by seemingly infallible integration of interviews, archival footage and new footage. You will never forget this story - or this man.
