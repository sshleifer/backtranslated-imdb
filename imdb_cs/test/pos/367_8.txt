Rating: 8 out of 10. Director: Alfred Hitchcock. Tennis star Guy Haines (Farley Granger) meets mysterious Bruno Anthony (Robert Walker) on the train. Soon after, Guy finds himself in the psychotic environment of Bruno's world. Guy is separated from his wife and is now involved in the work of a senator's daughter. Guy is interested in entering politics after he leaves tennis. Bruno wants to kill his own father, but he doesn't want to do it himself. Bruno suggests that he has Guy's wife, while Guy's part is to remove Bruno's father. Guy rejects this idea because he is not interested in killing his unpleasant wife. Bruno continues his half of the proposal. While Guy becomes the main suspect in the murder of his wife. As law enforcement continues to investigate Guy, Bruno continues to torment Guy, wanting him to complete the second half of the proposal. 'Strangers on the Train' has one of the most exciting endings to all Hitchcock movies.
