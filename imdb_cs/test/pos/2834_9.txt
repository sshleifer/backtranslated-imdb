Gregory Peck and Gig Young compete for the same girl, and after Peck sends Young to a very dangerous mission, they blame him for his reasons. Feeling guilty, Peck goes almost impossible to defend the fortress, where they are outnumbered by Indians. Peck decides on this mission of soldiers, which he considers to be the dirt of the country, and the actors playing these soldiers, Ward Bond, Lon Chaney Jr., Neville Brand among others, are excellent. The screenplay is derived from Charles Marquis Warren's novel, a Western specialist, as a writer, director and producer. The idea of ​​using this type of men as heroes has inspired many of the films that came out later, including "The Dirty Dozen" of 1967.
