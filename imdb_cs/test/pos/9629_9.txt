The Patriot is a well-thought-out, well-produced film that pulls the viewer directly into the story and keeps it to the end. It is true that it is not in the same spirit as Under Siege, et. al., but this is definitely a trademark film of what might be described as Seagal's second era of movies. Not so much action movies with a nod to the story that are fine, but a good story, with a point, and imprinted by some of Seagal's incredible action sequences. Is this the kind of movie that hardcore action junkies will have? Probably not. For those of us who not only like our actions, but also appreciate a well-told story, it is a big step forward for Seagal. It's almost like the transformation Clint Eastwood did when he tried to step out of the shadow of his "Dirty Harry" series. If he continued in the singular direction, they would both devote themselves to a task and guarantee the conciseness of their career ...
