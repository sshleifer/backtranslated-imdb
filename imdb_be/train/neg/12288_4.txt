Making the film as dark and visually inaccurate as it is possible to hide the budget deficits is often used strategy in the low-budget horror films, but this one takes it too far. It is so poorly lit and is cloudy (and this is almost entirely at night, to boot), you often end up almost looking at a black screen (though, perhaps, a bad video transfer may also have something to do with it). Unfortunately, the "murky" as the best word to describe the plot of the film. The filmmakers throw in different (and unoriginal) horror ideas without any semblance of logic, and halfway through you get the feeling that they are just about abandoned efforts to make a good horror film; You know, when you see the characters that are supposed to be in danger of death (or, in some cases, even the dead), making small talk .... (* 1/2)
