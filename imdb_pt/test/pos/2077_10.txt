I've seen this version of Hamlet on television for many years, and I've seen all the other versions since, be it television or film. However, this is what remains the truest representation of history for me. The excellent Derek Jacobi made Hamlet * real * to me. Before seeing this version, Shakespeare was simply grumpy to me and I never tried to understand Elizabethan English. Having seen Jacobi's Hamlet several times, it not only increased my knowledge of literature, but also that of my family. I promptly checked the play out of the Library and read, and poured over the recording accompanying it. Jacobi's interpretation drew me to a deeper knowledge. And yet, I've been struggling for a video of him for years and years without success. It gets a very high rating from the viewers. Why, then, was not released on video? It's the only Hamlet I would invest in ...
