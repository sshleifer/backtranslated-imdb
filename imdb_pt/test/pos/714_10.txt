I've never given a movie ten in ten before, but this is the closest I've ever gotten (I gave it a 9). There are very few movies that I really love, this however is one of them. With its brave realism, fantastic on-location location, and it's a great soundtrack that literally blew my young mind when I first saw it in 1979. At that time I did not know about prisons, violence, racial tensions, or the struggle to survive & live Free. I doubt anyone who is an adult, or in case someone who is growing up in the world today could be impacted by this movie the same way I was all those years ago, but I'll say this: "if you do not has seen this movie I envy you "; this is because you get a chance to see this great movie for the first time. For each of us they are different, but here are the rarest movies of all: the ones we really love ...
