As a previous commentator, I saw him in 1980 and have never been able to shake the memory of the thrilling story, splendid acting and dramatic soundtrack. It certainly contains some of Sam Waterston's best works. He and the writers portray Oppenheimer not only as an unjustly victimized hero - what he was - but also as naive, in love with alcohol and snobbery, a round portrait rather than a stereotype.
