There are very few movies that are able to tell such a complicated story on so many levels as well as Mishima: A Life in Four Chapters. One of the most difficult aspects of storytelling is the ability to rewind and move forward without losing the rhythm of the movie. This film not only flashes back and room with the largest of easy, but also flows through some of Yukio Mishima's greatest stories. This film surpasses in every way and is a joy to watch. Not to mention the incredible soundtrack of Philip Glass.
