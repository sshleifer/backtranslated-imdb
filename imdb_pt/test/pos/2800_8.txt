"L'Auberge Espagnole" gathered the audience wherever it was shown. Gathered audience awards at many film festivals around the world. And this is not strange. We have the ability to watch a joyful and amazing piece of art. And he is wise in the way. "L'Auberge Espagnole" is a very funny comedy about youth and growth. But above all, it is about the lights and shadows of living in the European Union. <br /> <br /> The main character of the film is a French student of economics Xavier. For your future carrier is sent for a year of study to Barcelona. In Spain, the lectures are given in Catalan. This probably does not help increase knowledge. But it helps to strengthen the relationships within the group of foreign students. Especially if they rent a large apartment together. There are 3 girls: English, Belgian and Spanish, plus three boys: German, Danish and Italian. Our French guy will also get there. A year is a long time. Long enough to get close and make friends. And know some European stereotypes at the same time that we separate them. <br /> <br /> Klapisch treats this special case of a process of union of Europe with humor and without pecky didatismo. He comes away from the idea that young people are everywhere in the same way. They like jokes. They like to make irresponsible relationships. But they do not neglect their aspirations. What is most interesting is the sum of the experience of this small community. They live together in the fire of daily chores, struggling with the surrounding reality. They are full of unusual ideas for life. Young Europeans return to their own countries to devote themselves to the life of an adult on their own. They are Europe's hope for tackling the Union's many problems. For example, the terrifying administration system. In the end, they prove that not only can they communicate and make friends despite the many differences. But they also now how to live their whole lives. And they will not let you take that life out of their lives.
