This film was made by Daiei Studios, known for its films Gamera. It's about a samurai who was murdered by one of his own men. He claims his throne, forcing his two sons to flee to the forest, where they hide near a huge stone statue for 10 years. In that time, the new Lord Samurai proved to be very brutal and ruthless in regard to the people of the village and the valley. So it seems that the only hope of good people is the stone statue, which is where a demon god sleeps; they want God to help them. <br /> <br /> This samurai movie brings to us traditional Japanese aspects, including sword fights, geishas and worshipers. It is a superb and powerful story of survival and hope, with the protagonists trying to triumph over pure evil. It is full of excitement, particularly the parts where children struggle to stay hidden while the evil warlord tries to reach them. In addition, it has a beautiful cinematography, with lush landscapes of the village and countryside - it instantly resembles ancient times in Japan. As with most samurai films such as "Os Sete Samuari" and " The Last Samuarai, "this film is no less than pure, sometimes graphic, action. There are several disturbing scenes in the movie. So it's not the casual science fiction movie. However, it is strong and powerful, and conveys a message that a well-meaning human can outdo any adversary as described in this movie, even the innocent young woman can calm the wrath of the demon god. The scenes of the demonic god, known as Daimajin, trampling his enemies and anything in his path instantly will remind him of a Godzilla or Gamera movie. Overall a powerful and serious but hopeful film. <br /> <br /> So be careful with your samurai sword. You would not want to rattle Daimajin's cages. Grade A
