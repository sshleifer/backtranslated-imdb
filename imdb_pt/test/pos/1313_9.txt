Mishima: Life in four chapters is an art biography about Yukio Mishima, a celebrated Japanese writer who bears a resemblance to Paul Schrader's previous character Travis Bickle (Taxi Driver): both are lonely people looking for their place in their society and when they realize that the world does not need them, they try to destroy the universe around them. <br /> <br /> If you want to learn about the life of Mishima then you will not find much information here because it shows that he did not live a very interesting life (except for his last day) but if you want understand his personality, then it is the best film of the genre, since most of the film is adapting his novels and also provides a guide to his thoughts. This film shows that Mishima was a person, who witnessed the downfall of Japanese culture, which he liked very much and with his final act, he tried to save the traditions and prove to himself that he is a true warrior, but he realized that as a person he was just a man with no power to change events.
