"Watch the Skies" (2005 - 60 minutes) is an excellent documentary on science fiction films. It was produced and directed by the critic Richard Schickel, author of more than 20 books on this subject. Mark Hamill is the documentary narrator. Schickel joins directors such as Steven Spielberg, James Cameron, George Lucas and Ridley Scott for a journey through time and space to showcase some of the most memorable science fiction films of the 1950s and also some more recent classics. The documentary shows six different approaches: the paranoia of atomic warfare; Fantastic trips to the moon; The enigmatic planet Mars; Good and bad aliens; The post-apocalyptic world; and the future of humanity. Features commentaries and scenes from the following classics: Flying Discs, Rocketship XM, Moon of Destiny, Children of Space, 20,000 Fathom Beast, Godzilla: King of Monsters, The Incredible Shrunken Man, Attack of the Assassin Tomatoes, A Journey to the Moon , The Raging Red Planet, The Forbidden Planet, The Thing Of An Other World, Land Against The Flying Disks, I Married A Monster From Outer Space, Invasion Of The Thieves Of Bodies, The Bubble, The War Of The Worlds, The Day The Earth Has Stopped , ET: The Extra Terrestrial, The Omega Man, The Planet of the Apes, The Terminator, 2001: A Space Odyssey, Things to Come and Metropolis. Highly recommended for science fiction fans!
