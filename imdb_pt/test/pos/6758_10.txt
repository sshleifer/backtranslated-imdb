Straight from the brilliant mind of animation pioneer Wladyslaw Starewicz, "Fétiche", or "The Mascot", as it is known today, remains a masterpiece of stop motion animation that, unfortunately, has almost been forgotten. However, this man's work deserves to be seen and in fact must be seen to be believed, because the excellent way that animation flows is simply unbelievable. Decades before "Toy Story," Starewicz conceived the idea of ​​moving toys, in "Fétiche," he tells the story of a little stuffed dog that befriends the sick daughter of its creator . One day his creator takes him to sell him, and the adventure begins when he tries to find his way home. In his Odyssey, he will travel from Paris to Hell and find the other toys that should be sold with him. <br /> <br /> It is a surreal experience to watch this movie, as Starewicz makes every imaginable creature alive with grace and beauty. The other toys include a beautiful ballerina, who loves a thief, but she is also secretly loved by a clown, forming a love triangle; an old woman, a plush cat and a stuffed monkey complete the group. every toy is so detailed and very expressive that without words one can understand its motives. <br /> <br /> In fact, the surreal atmosphere of all the animation is remarkable. It is hard to believe that such a work was done in 1934, since it looks even better than most current animations. The influence he has on the modern entertainer like Tim Burton and Henry Selick is very significant. <br /> <br /> This short is a masterpiece of animation and the remarkable work of a genius who did it all by himself and who has influenced animators for decades. Starewicz's work is an immortal work of art that must be seen by all.
