What attracts a man to military service? What prepares a man to survive brutal torture as a prisoner of war? What despair does it take to plan and execute an escape into the jungles of Southeast Asia? How does he handle the ghosts and memories that he returns home? Herzog tries to answer these questions in his documentary about Dieter Dengler, a German emigrant and US Navy pilot shot down over Laos in 1966, who was arrested, tortured and starving, but escaped to be haunted by experience for the rest of his life. . A powerful and personal first-hand account about the man, his life and experience reported through the insossible integration of interviews, filming footage and new images. You will never forget this story - or this man.
