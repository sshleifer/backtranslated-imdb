From beginning to end, this 1926 classic from Hal Roach Studios seems to sum up what was fun in the 1920s. Starring Charley Chase and now directed by the legendary Leo McCarey, who was unknown but would earn his living with Roach and would become pasture Greener in the 30s and 40s. Recently released on video and disc, this is one of the top ten examples of silent screen comedy and should be seen by audiences of all ages. Although today his star has virtually declined, Charley Chase was considered the leader in the short comedy field in the last years of the silences. He assisted the careers of Stan Laurel and Oliver Hardy before they were assembled as a team, Leo McCarey and a host of other talents. It is a pity he is remembered today. Check out this little gem of a movie. Once you do, you will be looking for other movies from this classic comic book. He had his hand in over 300 films and many of them survive. Rediscover this lost giant from a movie from a bygone era and its giant star.
