Making the film as bleak and visually fuzzy as possible to cover up budget deficiencies is a strategy often used in low-budget horror movies, but this takes it too far. It's so dimly lit and obscure (and it happens almost entirely at night) that you often end up looking at a black screen (though maybe the bad video transfer might have had something to do with it). Unfortunately, "obscure" is also the best word to describe the plot of the film. Filmmakers cast lots of (rather than original) ideas of terror without any semblance of logic, and halfway down you have the feeling that they abandoned the effort to make a good horror movie; you know this when you see characters who should be in mortal danger (or, sometimes, even dead) making small talk ... (* 1/2)
