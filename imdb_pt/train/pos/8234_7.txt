"Fido" is to be commended for having a weary genre, zombies, and turn it into a more original cinematic experience. The atmosphere of the early 50's is impressive, the acting is great and the whole production shows a lot of careful planning. Suddenly, the viewer is immersed in a world of gorgeous classic cars, dressed "Eisenhower era", art deco furniture and zombie servants. It would be very easy to dismiss "Fido" as a fluff like a cartoon, similar to "Tank Girl", but the two films are very different. "Fido has a structure, a script that tells a story and acting that is superior. Make no mistake, this is a daring black comedy that gets where so many others have failed.
