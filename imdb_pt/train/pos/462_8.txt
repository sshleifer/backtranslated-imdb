"The Lujin Defense" is, in the foreground, a story of an idiosyncratic savant of chess (Turturro) who becomes consumed by the game and, in the background, through flashbacks, his childhood life and the forces that created the man he it became. Factor in good and evil in the forms of his love interest (Watson) and former "mentor" (Wilson), respectively, and you have a melancholic drama full of pungent and delicately humorous moments mixed with the rich scenic beauty of Lake Como in Italy. intensity of the high chess game. An excellent film for those in period dramas, the author's euphemistic chess metaphor for orgasmic pleasures is an indication of the subtle wit behind the film.
