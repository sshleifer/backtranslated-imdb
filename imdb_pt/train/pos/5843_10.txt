Last production of Lubitsch, but not his least interesting film. Somehow ignored by critics, as he could not finish by himself and as the film was not co-signed by Preminger, he did most of the staging ... A very strange mix of musical (a remembrance of The Merry Widow?) And classic Lubitsch touch sentimentality (an impossible love story like Cluny Brown) still a very clever and intelligent, but not to be understood as some nostalgia for some lost world, but rather a testament on eternal feelings prevailing over the madness of humanity and especially men in times of war with a "moral" lesson still true today as it was in 1948. Billy Savage as a response to Preminger who lamented Lubitsch's funerals about having lost a great man replied that we still had his movies and that summarizes everything about that lady in the ermine ...
