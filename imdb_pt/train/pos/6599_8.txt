On the outskirts of São Paulo, the dysfunctional and hypocritical low-middle-class family of Teodoro (Giulio Lopes), Cláudia (Leona Cavalli) and teenage soninha (Sílvia Lourenço) have deep secrets. The religious Teodoro is in fact a gunslinger, hired to kill people in the neighborhood with his friend Waldomiro (Ailton Grace). He has a lover, the very devout woman Terezinha (Martha Meola), and he wants to regenerate, going to the field with her. Claudia has a young lover, Julio (Ismael de Araújo), who delivers meat at her father's butcher's shop. Soninha is a common teenager of sixteen years of the periphery, having active sexual life, smoking grass and loving heavy metal. When Julius is killed and neutered in his neighborhood, the lives of family members change. <br /> <br /> "Contra Todos" is a great Brazilian low budget movie that portrays life on the periphery of a great Brazilian. City. The story is very real, it uses the usual elements of the poor area of ​​the big Brazilian cities (traffickers, killers, evangelical religious fanatics, teenagers without hope etc.), has many remarkable points and an amazing ending, and the characters have excellent performances, acting in a natural way and making the story totally believable. The camera follows the characters, giving a great dynamics to the film. In the extras of the DVD, director Roberto Moreira explains that his script had no lines, just the description of the situations, and was partially released only a week before the start of the shootings. The actors have training in workshops and they used a lot of improvisation, being the reason for such a natural performance. My vote is eight. <br /> <br /> Title (Brazil): "Against All" ("Against All")
