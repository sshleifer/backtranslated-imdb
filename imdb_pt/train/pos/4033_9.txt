Many reviewers have pointed out that Pinjar is different from the production of Bollywood films. While this is true, Bollywood films are generally targeted to a specific audience and should be appreciated for accomplishing their goals in that regard. <br /> <br /> However, Pinjar is an excellent film for those looking for a change from the normal. Bollywood film based on equations. Defined during the partition between India and Pakistan, Pinjar focuses on a Punjabi girl who becomes a victim of social and cultural attitudes toward the treatment of women in her time. Paro, the protagonist, is forced to choose between a life with a man who kidnapped her and the fleeting hope of a life with his family back to the Indians ruled in Punjab. More than a matter of Hindus and Muslims, Pinjar approaches and defines the role of a woman as daughter, wife and mother in India and Pakistan in 1947. Unlike typical Bollywood films that are escapists in nature, Pinjar is a film that makes your audience contemplate these issues during and after the movie.
