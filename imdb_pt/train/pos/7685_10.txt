Another hit by Norman Lear detailing the problems that African Americans had to undergo in the turbulent 1960s and 1970s. With Esther Rolle and her husband along with three children living in a Chicago building in one predominantly black building In the neighborhood, the program showed what blacks were going through with a landlord (black agent, Mr. Bookman), as well as the day-to-day prices and problems of existence. The three children described how people seem to view their problems differently - from comedian JJ to militant Ralph Carter, to the daughter who also aspired to success, this program was a perfect description of the African-American life.
