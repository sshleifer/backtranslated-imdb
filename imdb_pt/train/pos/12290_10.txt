The Hotel Du Nord is a thrilling drama of guilt in which Marcel Carne portrays an amusing story of ill-fated love that also functions as a revolt against the cruel world. The film is based entirely on a pair of unlucky lovers. Pierre and Renee were mistaken. when they believed that suicide would put an end to their misery. The Hotel Du Nord has its own inimitable charm, as its inhabitants have become an essential part of the establishment. There is an element of union when everyone meets at the Hotel Du Nord to eat, talk. etc. Marcel Carne remained true to the spirit of the films produced in the 30s and 40s, as the Hotel Du Nord has a certain kind of nostalgic feeling. Carne, while re-creating the life of the Parisian roads was able to create a kind of nostalgia for black and white that gives a unique genre of poetic realism to its work. The Hotel Du Nord can be denominated like a quintessence of the cinematographic populism. The scene of the July 14 Ball on the banks of the Saint Martin Canal remains a magnificent sequence. Mense popularity can be judged from the fact that the Hotel Du Nord was declared as a national monument.
