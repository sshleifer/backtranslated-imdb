Hugh (Ed Harris) is a bachelor senator determined to run for president. One day, however, he encounters an old schoolmate named Aggie. Aggie (Diane Keaton) is a gifted and award-winning author with a lovely face and an independent spirit. Hugh is in love. He convinces Aggie to become her fiancé. But will Aggie have to sacrifice her principles of honesty in the world of politics where things are not always what they seem to be? And will it be able to withstand the rigors of a tough media campaign? This is, for the most part, a good novel for those who love stories of affection. Hugh and Aggie are absolutely in love and their games and conversations are a good sight. However, although the film tries to show political life in its reality, it is not completely successful. Nevermind. The production values ​​are high and the script is written very elegantly. With these advantages and the beautiful characters of Keaton and Harris, those who sit for the movie will find it a good entertainment.
