"The Godfather," "Citizen Kane," "Star Wars," "Good Fellows." None of the above compares to the complex brilliance of "The Sopranos." Each and every character has layers and layers of absolute truth, completely and totally three-dimensional. We worry about Tony Soprano wholeheartedly, despite the fact that in the simpler model of good versus evil, he is evil. Soprano is the most provocative, intricate and fascinating protagonist ever created for this point in history. If you are willing to be openly challenged as a viewer, and to be forever changed in your feelings about entertainment, watch "The Sopranos." I challenge anyone to sit and watch the first episode of the first season, and do not want to continue with the series. Each season is completely bright in its own way.
