Incessantly gloomy and depressing, the film evokes, just as it could be, the legendary misery and emptiness that characterized Houellebecq's controversial novel of the same name. Like many French films, his manner is melancholy, but is it painfully slow - or should it be, slowly, painful? Although this is an excellent and challenging film, it is not enjoyable and it is difficult to think at any time that someone might be in the "right" mood to see it.
