"Dressed to Kill" is certainly one of the best horror / suspense films ever made. It's a tense, elegant mix and extremely full of suspense of sex and violence. The acting is very good, the orchestral score of Pino Donaggio is unforgettable and there is many surprises to keep suspense fans intrigued. "Dressed to Kill" is a murder mystery involving a sexually frustrated housewife (Angie Dickinson), her teenage son (Keith Gordon), her psychoanalyst (Michael Caine) and a high-priced girl (Nancy Allen The Killer In The Movie is a transsexual named Bobbi, who is also one of Caine's patients. The film is full of breathtaking moments: the infamous elevator murder scene is extremely elegant and quite bloody too.
