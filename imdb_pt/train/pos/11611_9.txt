Japan 1918. The story of 16-year-old Ryu begins with the death of his father. As will be revealed later, both parents died of tuberculosis. In this desperate situation Ryus aunt arranged a marriage with a Japanese man in Hawaii, whom they know only from his image. Upon arriving in Hawaii Ryu learns that her new husband is much older than in photography, and that he lives in very humble circumstances next to a sugarcane plantation in which he works. Ryu is not accustomed to the hard work in the plantation and, desperate with the situation in his new house, thinks of running away. She soon discovers she has nowhere to go. Friendship with Kana, her co-worker, gives her new hope and strength. This image is based on actual events between 1907 and 1920 when thousands of Asian women married men in America who only knew of its image. This not well known image is well written and executed. The location is breathtaking. This film also features Mifune Toshiro on his last screen appearance as a Benshi (silent film narrator). This film gives an idea of ​​Japanese culture here and across the ocean. A must see!
