Beautiful story of Wisconsin native Dan Jansen and his real life, distressing fight to win the 1994 Olympic gold medal in Speed ​​Skating despite his overwhelming emotional loss with the death of his much loved best friend and family member ; his sister, Jane. <br /> <br /> The main focus of this story is to portray with sensitivity the actual emotional turmoil of pain, the one that feels when losing a special Beloved, and the struggle to cope productively and overcome the great loss! It is the incredible story of Dan Jansen's disgust at losing his beloved sister to leukemia, his struggle to deal with the intensity of his pain, while still maintaining his dream of winning the Olympics, and his final triumph in winning the gold medal in speed Skating for America and in honor of the memory of his sister; fulfilling her childhood promise to Jane! Following Dan Jansen's remarkable gold medal at the Winter Olympics in Lillihamer, Norway, he set up a foundation in 1994 to help fight leukemia, which cost his sister's life. and support youth sports programs, educational awards, and scholarships. His Foundation Dan Jansen promotes the philosophy that "as his father always told him at a young age:" there is more to life than skateboarding in circles! Keeping a proper perspective is the key! So, too, is setting goals and fulfilling dreams by persevering, overcoming adversity and never giving up! "Recommended for those who have already suffered the great loss of someone very special and loved in their lives! And for those who still believe in the realization of dreams and never give up!
