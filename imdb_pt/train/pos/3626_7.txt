This effort is based on the true story of Jim Morris, a high school science / baseball coach, who is inspired by his players to experience the pros and realize his dream of playing in major tournaments. Dennis Quaid, who's no stranger to sports movies, plays Morris with enough conviction to play the role, and producers do a dependable job of re-creating the real-world events that led to Morris as a prize-winner for the pitiful Tampa Bay Devil Rays . . The first half of the movie, dealing with your group of high school baseball players (everyone who seems too old to be in high school) is less effective and probably a little too long. Overall, the movie suffers from some rhythm problems and some extra subplots that we probably could not. However, it is still a rather engaging movie with an inspirational theme that proves once again that baseball is the national pastime for a reason. SERIE B-
