Watch the Skies (2005 - 60 Minutes) is a great documentary on Science Fiction films. It was prepared and staged by critic Schickel, author of more than 20 books on this topic. Mark Hamill is a documentary narrator. Schickel joins the directors with Steven Spielberg, James Cameron, George Lucas and Ridley Scott to make time and space to show some of the most memorable films of the fifties and some of the newest classics. The documentary shows six different approaches: the atomic war paranoia; Fantastic Trips to the Moon; Mars' mysterious planet; Good and evil alien; Apocalyptic World; and the future of mankind. It presents comments and scenes from the following classics: Flying Saucers, Rocketship XM, Destination Moon, Space Children, The Beast from 20,000 Fathoms, Godzilla: King of Monsters, Incredible Shrinking Man, Killer Tomato Attack, Trip to the Moon, Angry Red Planet, Forbidden Planet, Thing from the other world, Earth vs flying furnaces, I married a monster from Outer Space, invasion of body snatchers, stain, war of worlds, Earth is still alive: Extra Earth, Omega Man, Monkey Planet, Terminator, 2001: Space Missions, Things what comes and Metropolis. Highly recommended for Science Fiction fans!
