Well done drama based on Arthur Miller's novel. Something as simple as a couple of glasses becomes life changing. Lawrence Newman (William H. Macy) is a man who has chosen to be content with his daily life; the same work for twenty years and still lives with her mother. His boss told him to improve his vision with the glass pair. Newman's life changes drastically and throws him into hell. The glasses he chose make him look at the Jew. He loses his job and becomes the subject of difficult controls in the Brooklyn neighborhood. While looking for a job, he meets an attractive and pronounced Gertrude (Laura Dern) who lives in conflict because of his Jewish appearance. Soon a couple of new lives will become a horror full of humiliation and attacks. Very suitable feature: Joseph Ziegler, Peter Oldring, Kay Hawtrey and musical celebrity, Meat Loaf.
