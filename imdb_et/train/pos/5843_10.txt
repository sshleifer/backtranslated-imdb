Lubitsch's last production, but not his least interesting film. Critics ignored somehow because he could not finish it himself and did not sign the film for Preminger, who made most of the production ... A very strange musical mix (Merry Widow's memory?) And classic Lubitsch touches sentimentalism (impossible love story like Cluny Brown), but a very smart and intelligent but not a lost nostalgia for the lost world, but rather a testimony to the stupidity of mankind, and especially of men, in the eternal feelings of war, where the "moral" lesson still applies today, as it was in 1948.
