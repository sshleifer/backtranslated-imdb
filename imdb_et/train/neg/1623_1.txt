Supporting TV series for a popular author's work does not guarantee success. Yorkshire Television learned this hard when they bought the rights credited to Dick Francis in 1979, three of which were sent under the collective title 'The Racing Game'. Mike Gwilym was Sid Halley, a former jockey turned to a private eye after an accident where he lost his right hand just to replace it with artificial. Gwilym suffered from the lack of acute charisma (and seemed like one of the bad guys), while Mick Ford (who played the irritating Chico Barnes) made me think of the horse's ass when he was on the screen. Within six weeks, it asked less than a dynamic duo on the terrain, fixing unpleasant parcels to fix races, usually by the same methods - blackmail, kidnapping, or doping horses. Yorkshire Television cashed at the show, but without results. Violent, sexist, far-fetched and repetitive;
