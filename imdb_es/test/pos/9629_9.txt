The Patriot is a well thought-out and well-produced film, which will directly draw the viewer to the story and keep it involved until the end. Of course, it is not in the same line as Under Siege, et. al., but this is definitely the feature film of what could be described as the second era of Seagal movies. Not so much action movies with a nod to a story released in good measure, but a good story, combined with a point, and injected with some of Seagal's inimitable action sequences. Is this the kind of movie that hardcore action addicts will enjoy? Probably not. But for those of us who not only like our action, but we can also appreciate a well told story, this is a great step forward for Seagal. It's almost like the transformation Clint Eastwood did when he tried to get out from under the shadow of his "Dirty Harry" series. Either of them, if he had continued in the singular direction in which they were headed, would have been locked up forever in a specific role and guaranteed the brevity of their careers ...
