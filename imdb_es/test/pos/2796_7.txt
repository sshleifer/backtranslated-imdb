The film by Cedric Klapisch, L'AUBERGE ESPAGNOLE, is an easy and cheerful charm, wrapped in the nostalgia of our younger years and attractive youngsters. At its core, it is the long-running presentation of the long-running soap opera MTV reality series known as "The Real World," in which, as its slogan says: "This is the" real story "of seven strangers chosen to live in a house and have your lives recorded ... so watch what happens when people stop being educated and start being real. " This is exactly what happens, minus the cameras placed in every tiny corner of the house in Barcelona, ​​Spain, where Xavier (Romain Duris) comes to stay, and has to learn Spanish to meet the requirements of his work. Strange in many ways, slowly forming a camaraderie with his housemates who come from all corners of Europe, except the United States ... this is a film in which the only American who is shown is an unpleasant character with whom Wendy ( the adorable Kelly Reilly) is having an affair with ("Only for sex," she confesses, since she has her own boyfriend who makes a late but bewildered appearance). Throughout his stay there, he tries to maintain a long distance relationship with his girlfriend played by Audrey Tautou while he starts a tentative flirting with the wife of the owner of the Spanish house where he stays and receives some advice from a lesbian house. -mate (Cecile de France) on how to seduce a woman. A small sweet feature that presents a moment in time that twenty-somethings will never see again, L'AUBERGE ESPAGNOLE is an unforgettable fun that contains within itself the threshold to the "real world" experienced through the eyes of Xavier.
