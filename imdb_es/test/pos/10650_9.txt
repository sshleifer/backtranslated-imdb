This 1925 film tells the story of the mutiny aboard the battleship Potemkin in the port of Odessa. The film celebrated the twentieth anniversary of the 1905 uprising, which was seen as a direct precursor to the October Revolution of 1917. Following its mounting theory, Eisenstein plays with the scenes, their duration and the way they combine to emphasize their Message, in addition to Use different camera shooting angles and revolutionary lighting techniques. The sequence "Steps of Odessa" in Potemkin is one of the most famous in the history of cinema. The baby stroller that was released by the steps after his mother was shot was recreated later in The Untouchables of Brian d 'Palma. It is clear that the film is one of the best that has been made, considering its moment and how innovative it was, although it takes a bit of patience and being a true enthusiast of the movies to cover its 70 minutes.
