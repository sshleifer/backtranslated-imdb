The only impression of CHIKAMATUS MONOGATARI that I could find was abysmal, I could hardly see it. Which is a shame, since it is among the best Mizoguchi movies. The story, which I think had been done before and since then by other Japanese directors, is a little clearer than my favorite Mizoguchi movies (SANSHO THE BAILIFF and UGETSU MONOGATARI), and is essentially a tragic romance story, in this case a transgressive romance that crosses strict class boundaries. As always with Mizoguchi, there is a tone of defiance exquisitely expressed, and, despite the bad impression, I was very satisfied. As with all Mizoguchi movies, I am eagerly waiting for a restored DVD release, whenever possible ...
