Angela (Sandra Bullock) is a computer expert but, being shy and somewhat reclined, she does all her work from the confines of her condo. Just as she is about to take a vacation in Mexico, a co-worker sends her a computer disk with disturbing information. Angela agrees to meet with her co-worker, but he dies mysteriously in a plane crash. Angela goes to Mexico but takes the album with her. While she is sunbathing on the beach, an amazing looking gentleman named Jack (Jeremy Northam) approaches her. She falls in love with them and the two end up in a boat to Cozumel. However, Jack works for the people who generated the secret information on the disk and he wants to get it. Even after Angela escapes her clutches and lands in the USA. UU., Jack makes things difficult. Change the identity of Angela in each computer across the country, which makes her lose her condo, her bank account, everything. Can Angela, a computer genius, beat Jack at his own game? This very exciting movie has many assets. First of all, Bullock and Northam are two very beautiful and interesting actors, and their presence adds an immediate charm. The script is very intelligent and secure in its knowledge of the capabilities of computers and their relevance in today's world. The costumes, sets, production and direction of the film are also wonderful. And, despite how it sounds, there's a lot of exciting action when Angela goes racing to defeat her enemy. If you love thrillers without unnecessary bloodshed or violence, this is an excellent option. It offers turns and turns with great frequency, which makes it possible for the viewer to "reduce" a very good night of entertainment.
