What attracts a man to military service? What prepares a man to survive brutal torture as a prisoner of war? What desperation leads to the planning and execution of an escape to the jungles of Southeast Asia? How do you deal with the ghosts and memories with which you return home? Herzog tries to answer these questions in his documentary about Dieter Dengler, a German émigré and a US Navy pilot shot down over Laos in 1966, who was taken prisoner, tortured and starved, but eventually escaped to be persecuted for the rest of the experience of their life. . A powerful and personal first-hand account of the man, his life and experience, told through the unparalleled integration of interviews, archival material and new archival material. You will never forget this story, or this man.
