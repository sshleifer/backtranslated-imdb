As a previous commentator, I saw him in 1980 and I have never been able to shake the memory of the exciting story, the splendid performance and the dramatic music. It certainly contains some of Sam Waterston's best work. He and the writers represent Oppenheimer not only as a hero unjustly victimized, as he was, but also as naive, fond of alcohol and snobbish, a rounded portrait instead of a stereotype.
