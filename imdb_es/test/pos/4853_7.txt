Well performed drama based on a novel by Arthur Miller. Something as simple as a pair of glasses becomes a change of life. Lawrence Newman (William H. Macy) is a man who has chosen to be satisfied with his worldly life; The same job twenty years ago and still living with his mother. Your boss tells you to correct your vision with a pair of glasses. Newman's life changes drastically and he sinks into hell. The glasses he chose make him look Jewish. He loses his job and becomes the object of intense scrutiny in his Brooklyn neighborhood. Looking for work, she meets the attractive and outspoken Gertrude (Laura Dern), who lives with the conflict because of her Jewish appearance. Soon, the new life of the couple becomes a nightmare full of humiliations and attacks of intolerance. A very fit cast that features: Joseph Ziegler, Peter Oldring, Kay Hawtrey and musical fame, Meat Loaf.
