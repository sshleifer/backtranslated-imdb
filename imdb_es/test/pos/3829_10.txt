Unfortunately, the task becomes more difficult when one of the men accuses Spock of being allied with the enemy, due to the physical similarity between the Romulans and the Vulcans, two races that, in fact, are closely related (a fact that is pretty ironic). Although the Romulans are not really based on the Soviets (the name is actually taken from Romulus, the founder of Rome), the scenario is clearly inspired by the very lively fear that the American and Russian citizens had at the time that either of the two nations could be able to destroy the other with nuclear weapons (that fear gave rise to the titular concept of "balance of terror"). But even without the subtext, this remains an essential episode, due in large part to the intellectual battle between the two adversaries, which results in a duel between Shatner and Lenard.
