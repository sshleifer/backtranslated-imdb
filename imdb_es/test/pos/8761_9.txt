"Traffik 1989" is a six-part Emmy Award-winning miniseries from the United Kingdom, which was the inspiration for the Oscar-winning "Traffic 2000". The five-hour film breaks the opium / heroin trade for the viewer from the hand-tossing of poppy seeds in a field in Afghanistan to the "head fever" of an addict to the main line in an apartment in England. "Traffik" not only offers entertainment value through interspersed dramatic stories, but also provides an overview of the international drug trade at all levels, answering questions about who is, how and why of the age-old machine. unstoppable narcotics. Synergistically entertaining and educational, "Traffik" will be a time well spent for teenagers and seniors. (A)
