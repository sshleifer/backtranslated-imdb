Deepa Mehta's "Fire" is innovative, bold and artistic. A masterful social commentary on the plight of women in conservative indigenous homes of upper middle class, this is a film that no one should miss. Shabana Azmi and Nandita Das offer stellar performances while minimizing their characters as much as possible. ARKANSAS. The music of Rahman is the work of genius and almost plays the role of another character in the film. Mehta uses the Rahman score and, together, create such incredible sound montages that effectively portray the world views around Radha and Sita every time they seek support. This film is not about lesbianism as many have described it. Lesbianism is only part of the movie. It is unfortunate that most people tend to write the movie saying it is taboo instead of giving it a shot and seeing its true meaning.
