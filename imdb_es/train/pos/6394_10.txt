Carl Brashear (Cuba Gooding, Jr.) was born to sharecroppers in the deep south. He joins the navy, and then tells his father he will return. The father gives him an old radio, and Brashear leaves on the navy bus. The most valuable thing that his impassive father taught him was: "Never give up." After a recommendation from a white commander Powers Boothe, who admires his unit and his guts, he is sent to the Navy diving school in Bayonne, NJ. He endures harassment from his friends in uniform and from his trainer, Navy Chief Navy Diver, Billy Sunday (Robert De Niro), and from the commanding officer, named Pappy, (Hal Holbrook, who "has almost as many loose screws as an old car. "Everyone wants him to drop out of school, and the prejudice is pretty fierce.The dangers of diving are a new setback when he loses a leg due to an accident on board the ship.Despite this setback, he tells his wife that will train and achieve his goal, and with the help of Billy Sunday, (now both joined in commiseration for their sufferings), train and is able to become the first black diver in the Navy with his artificial limb despite the skepticism of a Big mockery and the dubious captain at the Navy Department hearing in Washington, DC to determine if it meets the criteria.An inspiring movie, proving that determination can surpass all odds.
