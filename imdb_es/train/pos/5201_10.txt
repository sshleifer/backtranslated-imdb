The Human Tornado is a Blaxploitation movie from the 70s starring nightclub comedian Rudy Ray Moore in perhaps his most endearing role to date. The film tells the story of Dolemite, a malefactor who gets on the wrong side of a white and racist sheriff when sleeping with his wife. Dolemite barely escapes, and travels to sunny California to visit an old friend, owner of a nightclub (and Madam to the "ladies" of Dolemite) called Queen Bee. However, it seems that the owner of a rival nightclub with Mob connections is trying to raid his racket, so Dolemite takes matters into his own hands. Rudy Ray Moore shows many diverse talents in this historical film, which includes strong dramatic skills, a mastery of Kung Fu, an impressive singing voice (provides two of the songs in the soundtrack), a touching and compassionate side with the ladies and a In general, convincing charisma and acute sense of comic timing. This movie has it all, people: a deep plot, an action full of blisters, a comedy of laughter per minute, beautiful women in distress, a slam-bang ending ... what more can you ask for in a movie?
