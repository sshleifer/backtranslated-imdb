The Last Command (1928) is a silent film directed by Josef von Sternberg. It shows the Tsarist General, the Grand Duke Sergio Alexander (Emil Jannings) in his glory days. In 1917 he had all the power, but after the revolution and the collapse. of imperial Russia has nothing. He also had the love of a woman, Natalie Dabrova (Evelyn Brent). Approximately ten years later, he requests a small part in a film about the revolution. His old enemy, Lev Andreyev (William Powell) is the director who decides whether or not to hire him as an extra film. The Last Command is a very good silent drama. Emil Jannings does a memorable job in the lead role. Evelyn Brent is wonderful as the protagonist. William Powell is great as always. There are many scenes to remember in this movie. Like many scenes with Jannings and Brent. And then there's the ending with Powell and Jannings. This is a movie that plays in many places.
