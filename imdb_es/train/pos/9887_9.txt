"Erendira" is a movie from Mexico that is rarely talked about. The film only exists in low quality VHS format. It's a pity that this movie has not been released on DVD. "Erendira" is stunning and beautiful with its realistic images of magic. "Erendira" is based on a story from the novel "100 years of solitude". Erendira daydreams and accidentally burns her grandmother's house. Her evil grandmother, played by Irene Papas, forces her to practice prostitution to pay for the damages. The whole town receives a piece of Erendira, so to speak. Although the subject sounds harsh, the film does not exploit sexuality. It is done in a mature artistic manner. The movie also has some incredible costumes. Some of the most surreal aspects of the film that stand out are the origami birds that are transformed into real birds and a golden orange with a diamond in the center. Erendira is an incredible movie, that even manages to throw in humor. This is definitely a movie that deserves a special release on DVD. As they said in Spanish, "It's very, very good! Excellent!
