"De vierde man" (The Fourth Man, 1984) is considered one of the best European thrillers of the eighties. This latest work by Dutch director Paul Verhoeven in his home country before moving to Hollywood to become a big star with films like "Total Recall", "Basic Instinct" and "Starship Troopers" is about a psychopathic and disillusioned author (Jeroen Krabbe) Go to the seashore to recover. There he meets a mysterious fatal woman (Renee Soultendieck) and begins a fatal romance with her. He becomes addicted to it with heart and soul and discovers that his three previous husbands died with mysterious circumstances ... <br /> <br /> "De vierde man" is very influenced by the old Hollywood movie and the psychopath. The thrillers of Alfred Hitchcock and Orson Wells. It takes a lot of time to create a dark and exciting atmosphere, and a few moments of extreme graphic violence have the right impact to make the story move forward. The suspense is sometimes almost unbearable and, at times, it recalls the works of the Italian cult director Dario Argento. <br /> <br /> The cast is also outstanding, especially the performance of Krabbe as a mentally disturbed writer who opened the doors to his international film career ("The Living Daylights", "The Fugitive").
