This friendly comedy faces a few bizarre characters. Especially, of course, the two main characters. Jack Lemmon plays Felix, a hypochondriac whose wife lost him because he could no longer stand his cooking and cooking attacks. So he tries to commit suicide but each attempt fails. Walter Matthau plays Oscar, his friend, an unreliable and unreliable sports reporter who lives divorced from his ex-wife in a singles apartment. He offers his anguished friend Felix a new home in his apartment. And soon the problem begins because two of these opposing characters can not live together for a long time. Felix turns Oscar's messy apartment into a clean exhibition floor. He cleans and cooks all the time. After a short time, Oscar feels mania persecution ... Filmed theatrically and excellently acted. Above all, Jack Lemmon's work is wonderful. He is the perfect clown. It makes us laugh but in a tragicomic way. Look for the wonderful scene when both men invite their two neighbors to dinner, because Oscar has to touch something softer than a bowling ball. While he prepares the drinks, Felix sits with the two ladies in the living room. To get out of this embarrassing situation, start talking about the weather. A minute later, he changes the subject and talks about his ex-wife and children. Suddenly he starts crying and when Oscar comes back with the drinks, there are three people crying in the living room. The film is full of scenes so funny and at the same time moving. An intelligent comedy, entertaining and with a lot of heart. 10 out of 10!
