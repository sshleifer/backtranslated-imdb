Hugh (Ed Harris) is a hotshot, a single senator determined to run for president. One day, however, he meets an old high school classmate named Aggie. Aggie (Diane Keaton) is a successful and award-winning author with a charming face and an independent spirit. Hugh is hurt. He convinces Aggie to become his fiancee. But will Aggie have to sacrifice her principles of honesty in the world of politics, where things are not always what they seem to be? And can she endure the rigors of a harsh media campaign? This is, for the most part, a good romance for those who love affection stories. Hugh and Aggie are absolutely in love and their jokes and conversations are a good view. However, although the film tries to show political life in its reality, it does not succeed. No matter. The production values ​​are high and the script is written very elegantly. With these advantages and the handsome characters of Keaton and Harris, those who sit down to the film will find it a good entertainment.
