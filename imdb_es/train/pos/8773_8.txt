Errol Flynn is "Gentleman Jim" in this 1942 film about boxer Jim Corbett, known as the man who beat John L. Sullivan. Directed with a light hand by Flynn's good friend, Raoul Walsh, the film also stars Alexis Smith, Jack Carson, Alan Hale Sr., William Frawley and Ward Bond. Flynn plays an ambitious and selfish young man who has a natural talent for boxing and is sponsored by the exclusive Olympic Club in San Francisco at the end of the 19th century. Although he is of good character, the fact that he is a "poor Irishman" and a social climber generates resentment in the members of the Olympic Club; most of them can not wait to see him lose a fight, and bet against him. Despite this, he rises to fame, even working as an actor. Finally, he has the opportunity he was waiting for, a match with the world champion, John L. Sullivan (Ward Bond). Sullivan demands a deposit of $ 10,000 to ensure that Corbett looks like he is fighting for the $ 25,000 purse. Corbett and his manager (Frawley) desperate to get the money.
