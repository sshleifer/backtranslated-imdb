Russ and Valerie are having discussions about how to start a family. The couple lives in an elegant apartment and runs an auction business that deals with valuable collectibles. At the same time, the owner of a dedicated adoption agency takes a mini-vacation and leaves the orphanage in charge of his father (Leslie Nielsen). Father Harry is in the rental business and has the brilliant idea of ​​"renting" some of the children from the orphanage to couples like Russ and Valerie. Harry, who realizes the couple's dilemma, offers a family of brothers for a 10-day rental period! Brandon, Kyle and Molly move into the apartment with their foster parents, with funny consequences, since the new caregivers have no experience with the children. But where is the possibility of a happy ending? This is a beloved family movie. The actors, including Nielsen as the wheel merchant and Christopher Lloyd as the apartment's friendly doorman, are wonderful. The script is agile and fun, and the production values ​​in general are quite high. Yes, if only life could be like that! Orphaned children everywhere deserve the opportunity to show that they are adorable and can give so much joy to parents who are considering adoption. If you want to show your family a movie that is based on good values ​​but is also very entertaining, find this movie. It is guaranteed that everyone laughs, even when their hearts are melting.
