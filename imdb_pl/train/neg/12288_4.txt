Making a movie as dark and visually fuzzy as possible to cover up budget deficiencies is a frequently used strategy in low-budget horror movies, but it takes too much time. It is so dimly lit and dark (and takes place almost entirely at night to run) that often ends virtually looking at the black screen (although maybe a bad video transfer may have something to do with it). Unfortunately, "dark" is also the best word to describe the plot of the film. Film makers introduce various (and non-original) ideas of horror with no semblance of logic, and in the middle you feel that they simply gave up the effort to make a good horror; you know it when you see characters who are to be in mortal danger (or, in some cases, even dead), talking ...
