As many people know, Mexican cinema was very poor after the so-called Golden Age of Mexican Cinema, fortunately in the late 90s and early 21st century, great films such as La Ley de Herodes, Bajo California, Amores Perros, and Y Tu appeared Mamá También and of course El Coronel No Tiene Quien le Escriba. El Coronel ... is a wonderful film that tells the classic story of Gabriel García Márquez, eliminating the elements of magical realism, and replacing them with a primitive reality living in Mexico, not only by people like a colonel who is waiting for their retirement, but by more than half of the population Of Mexico who live in complete poverty. The characters of the film satirically represent classic figures from the Mexican society, such as the nationalist colonel, cold and even ambitious priest, hypocrite, but at the same time faithful companion, tolerant and patient wife, hidden homosexual, etc. movie, you have to see if you want to learn more about Mexican society, and especially if you want to watch a great movie, by one of the best directors in Mexico
