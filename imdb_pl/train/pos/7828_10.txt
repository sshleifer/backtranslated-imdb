I show this film to university students in speech and media law, because his lessons are timeless: why speaking out against injustice is important and can lead to changes sought by the oppressed. Why freedom of the press and freedom of speech are essential for democracy. This is a compulsory tale of how apartheid drew the world's attention through the activism of Steven Biko and the journalism of Donald Woods. It is also an important lesson in freedom of speech: "You can blow out a candle, but you can not blow a fire. When the flame begins to catch, the wind will blow him higher. " (From Biko through Peter Gabriel to Shaking the Tree).
