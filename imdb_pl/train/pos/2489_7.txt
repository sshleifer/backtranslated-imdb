Hugh (Ed Harris) is a hot photographer, a bachelor senator, determined to run for president. One day, however, it happens that he goes to an old classmate named Aggie. Aggie (Diane Keaton) is an excellent and award-winning author with a charming face and independent spirit. Hugh is infected. He convinces Aggie to become his fiancé. But will Aggie have to sacrifice her integrity rules in the world of politics, where things are not always what they seem to be? Will it be able to withstand the rigors of an acute media attack? It is above all a nice affair for those who love stories about feelings. Hugh and Aggie are absolutely in love, and their banter and conversation is a good view. However, although the film tries to show political life in its reality, it does not succeed completely. Does not matter. Production values ​​are high, and the script is very elegantly written. Thanks to these qualities and the handsome characters of Keaton and Harris, those who sit down to the film will find good entertainment in it.
