It is sad to watch this movie now that we know how AKN has been affected by international capitalism. Biko died for nothing. Woods has not achieved much. Yes, apartheid was abolished, but the whole power apparatus was reserved by whites, leaving the ANC government more or less powerless. As Naomi Klein writes in the Shock Doctrine, in talks between black and white leaders, the "deKlerka government had a twofold strategy. First, by attracting the rising Washington consensus that there is not just one way to run the economy, he outlined the key sectors of making economic decisions - such as trade policy and the central bank --- as "technical" or "administrative", and then used many new political tools - international trade agreements, innovations in constitutional law and structural adjustment programs --- transferring control over these power centers to supposedly impartial experts, economists and officials from the IMF, the World Bank, GATT and the National Party - everyone except ANC liberation fighters . "Statistical results are frightening, there have been too many changes and AIDS is flourishing. Watching Cry Freedom in this light is deeply ironic - actually tragic. ANC has evolved from a solution into a basic problem.
