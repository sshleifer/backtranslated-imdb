"Fido" should be praised for taking the well-known genre, zombie and transforming it into the most original film experience. The atmosphere of the early 50's is stunning, great acting, and the whole production shows a lot of careful plans. Suddenly the viewer immerses himself in the world of beautiful classic cars, Eisenhower dresses, art deco furniture and zombie servants. It would be very easy to reject "Fido" as a similar to the cartoon fluff, similar to "Tank Girl", but these two films are very different. "Fido has a structure, a script that tells a story, and acting is better. Make no mistake, it's a bold black comedy that goes where others have failed. I would highly recommend.
