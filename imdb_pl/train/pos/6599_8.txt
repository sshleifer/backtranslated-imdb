On the outskirts of São Paulo, the very low-lying, middle-class dysfunctional and hypocritical family Teodoro (Giulio Lopes), Cláudia (Leona Cavalli) and teenager Soninha (Sílvia Lourenço) have profound secrets. Religious Teodoro is indeed a hit man, employed to kill people in the neighborhood with his friend Waldomiro (Ailton Graça). He has a lover, a very pious woman, Terezinha (Martha Meola), and wants to regenerate, going to the country with her. Cláudia has a young lover, Júlio (Ismael de Araújo), who delivers meat to the father's butcher's shop. Soninha is an ordinary sixteen-year-old teenager from the periphery, having an active sex life, smoking grass and loving heavy metal. When Júlio is killed and neutered in his neighborhood, the lives of family members change. <br /> <br /> "Contra Todos" is a great low-budget Brazilian film that depicts life on the outskirts of a large Brazilian city. History is very real, it uses the usual elements of the poor area of ​​the great Brazilian cities (drug dealers, hits, fanatical religious evangelicals, hopeless teenagers, etc.), has many plot points and a surprising end, and the characters have excellent performances, acting very naturally and making them the story is completely reliable. The camera follows the characters, giving the film great dynamics. In Extras of the DVD, director Roberto Moreira explains that his screenplay had no lines, only a description of the situation and was partially revealed only a week before the start of the shooting. The actors conduct training in workshops and used many improvisations, which are the reason for such a natural game.
