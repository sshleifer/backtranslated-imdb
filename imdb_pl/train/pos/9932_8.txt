If you go to this movie, expecting something that is not, you will be disappointed, like in any movie. This movie contains what Hemmingway described as the "iceberg effect". At first glance, it is simply the memory of random movie clips that were broken together to create a movie. If it were written in the book, it would be a short story, because the action in the movie is very fast and if you do not try to catch it, the reasoning of the story (along with a subtle announcement) may very well pass you by. Definitely a movie that you have to see twice to fully appreciate. Experimental cinematography barely describes this film. Working with the camera and post-production largely affect the overall taste of the film, making it quite artistic at some moments and open to interpretation in others (which is desirable in American films as late). Although in some parts it may be a bit rough, gruesome and too heavy for some viewers, the movie will never become completely unreal. The only aspect of the movie that I would write as "needs improvement" is the choice of the soundtrack.
