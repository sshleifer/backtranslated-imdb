Make the movie as dark and visually soft as possible in order to cover budget deficits is a frequent use of movie-driven movies, but this one takes it too far. It is SO poorly lit and quiet (and it takes place almost entirely at night, in boots) that you often end up normally looking at a black screen (although perhaps the bad video transfer may also have something to do with that). Alas, "quiet" is also the best word to describe the film. Filmists are thrown into diverse (and unoriginal) horror ideas without any pretense of logic, and the half you get the feeling that they just about abandoning the effort to make a good movie support; You know it when you see the character that is supposed to be deadly (or, on some occasions, even dying) to speak little .... (* 1/2)
