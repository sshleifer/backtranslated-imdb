This is undoubtedly the best performance of John Thaw, where he manages to eliminate any features of his character as Morse inspector and brings Tom's perfect adaptation from the pages of the book to the TV screen. This is a well-done production that keeps family vibrations in spite of very mature themes, such as the outbreak of World War II and the physical abuse of the child. However, the relationship between Tom and young Willie, which is the heart and soul of this story. It is thrilling and beautiful to see this connection between the evacuated boy in London and the terrible old man he has allowed him to develop - a true connection of grandfather / nephew. It is a pity that this story was not made with a larger budget, with a better director because it belongs to the big screen, which is not presented once or twice every ten years on a Sunday afternoon . With the right guidance, John Thaw will be celebrated worldwide and will award many awards for his brilliant performance in this film. A great actor and a great role that should have been honored more than he was at the time.
