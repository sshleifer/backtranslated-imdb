This movie was my first contact with Mr. Sica, so I did not know what to expect. But what I saw just broke my expectations - in a good way. The story is not complex and shows us the life of the orphan boy and, in particular, his connection to the poor people community who together have built a hood of simple blackboard houses. This small town in another city lives its own life and things are going well. But one day there is a water resource found, and a wealthy noble man is interested in buying the place. But, as the title of the film shows - there is a miracle that takes place. Our character becomes a great pigeon from his dead mother. What will he do to protect his friends and the whole built city? It was just a masterpiece of natural comedy. It shows the behavior of poor people and how money and property can talk and play with people. Also included are many fun moments and scenes - from the beginning with the place where the sun shines and much more.
