I remember going to 25 years in Spain, getting a lift from, what turned out to be, two little fugitives of Italian tailors. They were doing a lot out of the law, but on the other side wearing a small portrait of Jesus in their pocket to protect them ... Just unjust, good and bad, criminals and corrected where in a new combination, outside the categories who know them. "Les Valseuses" gives me, though a film and not a real life, a picture close to my own experiences: the intensity of each moment as soon as you go "totally behind" and you go to the most important moments, life form "dangerous" because, as we all know, there are people who are not prepared for it and who want to persecute you for "theft" and so on ... This movie touches the "values" is a story about "what is good and not": moral. It is the resurrection of the individual who fights against false morals and conformism ... There is danger to the end, because how far can you go with your own "freedom" and over your moral boundaries and those of other people? What to do with people who are willing to hurt you, put you in jail, or even shoot you for things you do, such as "stealing" gas from a multinational petroleum company for your car ? Les Valseuses re-awakens these questions in me, because morality, in contradiction with the usual media message, is quite complex ...
