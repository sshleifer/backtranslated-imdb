A sentimental story with a sentimental track. It's a little girl (with a voice impediment) who values ​​her green parrot Paulie. The parrot thinks and speaks as a human being and offers help and advice to his constant friend. The parrot is definitely the star of the film. Sometimes mischievous and sometimes depressed, the bird captures the state of mind in a remarkable and expressive way. Also, the synchronized voice is very well done and in a minute or two you can think that this intelligent bird exists. At the beginning of the film, his father gives the bird to a borrower and subsequent scenes the story of Paulie's constant struggle over many years to be reunited with his mistress. One of the many memorable scenes is when it falls into a bad company and is encouraged to spy people using ATMs. Paulie has a phenomenal memory. The end is predictable, but who else would want. Kids will love this movie and anyone who holds a bird like a pet will enjoy Paulie's antics.
