So, many times, with Stephen King's adaptations, you only get a character collection that recites the dialogue from the books. This really captures the heart of the book. Perhaps because it DOES NOT use large pieces of text directly from the book, but it is a bit more than an improvisation of the events in the story. Much of his success is Miko Hughes as Gage for Kids. Dale Midkiff and Denise "Tasha Yar" Crosby behaves with his parents. There is a scene where Louis feeds on Gage and is very natural together. Fred Gwynne is WONDERFUL. He perfectly keeps the emphasis of Maine without falling into parody and is wise and warm, just as Jud. (8 of 10)
