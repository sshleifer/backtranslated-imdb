It was very heart-warming. As an expatriate Frenchman, I was particularly glad to see this TV movie, which I think deserves a wider distribution. The act was good, and the ties between every member of this French rural family seemed so powerful and so natural. Undoubtedly, a wonderful movie about the concept of family and inclusion in homosexuality and AIDS. One of the most powerful aspects of the film is this privileged relationship between the eldest son (Leo) who contracted HIV and the youngest son (Marcel) to whom the rest of the family is trying to hide the situation. The two characters wake each other progressively as time passes for Leo, who is not willing to spend the rest of his life surviving drugs ...
