On the outskirts of São Paulo, the mid-class dysfunctional and hypocritical family, Teodoro (Giulio Lopes), Cláudia (Leona Cavalli) and teenager Soninha (Sílvia Lourenço) have profound secrets. Religious Teodoro is indeed a successful man committed to killing people in the neighborhood with his friend Waldomiro (Ailton Graça). He has a girlfriend, the very devoted woman Terezinha (Martha Meola), and wants to regenerate, going to the country with her. Cláudia has a young boyfriend, Júlio (Ismael de Araújo), who supplies meat for his father's butcher. Soninha is a sixteen-year-old teenager in the periphery, having an active sexual life, smokin grass and loving heavy metals. When Júlio is killed and castrated in their neighborhood, the life of family members changes. "Contra Todos" is a Brazilian film with a very small budget, illustrating life on the outskirts of a large Brazilian city. The story is very real, it uses the usual elements of the poor area of ​​the big Brazilian cities (drug dealers, hit people, fanatical evangelical people, hopeless teenagers, etc.), has many plot points and a surprising end, and the characters perform excellent, very natural and making the story totally credible. The camera follows the characters, giving a great dynamic to the movie. In the DVD Extract, director Roberto Moreira explains that his script had no lines but just a description of the situations and was partially revealed just a week before the shootings began. The actors have training in workshops and have used a lot of improvisations, being the reason for such a natural action. My vote is eight. <br /> <br /> Title (Brazil): "Contra Todos" ("Against All")
