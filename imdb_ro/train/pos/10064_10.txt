King of Masks is a beautiful story told that violates family gender preferences for men against human preference for love and for the company. Located in China in the 1930s, during a flood, we meet Wang, an elderly street performer, whose talent is magical and captivates the admiration of everyone who assists him. When a famous opera interpreter sees and then friends with Wang, he invites Wang to join his band. However, we learn that Wang's family tradition only allows him to pass on the secrets of a son. By learning that Wang does not have children, Wang is encouraged to find an heir before the magic is lost forever. Taking advice at heart, Wang buys an eight-year-old to fulfill his inheritance; will teach his new son, Doggie, the ancient art of silk masks. Soon, Wang discovers a fact about Doggie that threatens rare and dying art. Together, Wang and Doggie create a relationship and experience the range of emotions that invariably accompany him. The story is absorbing. The setting is serene and simply costly. In conclusion, it is a winning art film of the international prize, which can only move and inspire.
