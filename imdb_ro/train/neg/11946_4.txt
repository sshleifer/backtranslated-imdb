"Running Jumping & Standing Still Film" is not a movie as such, it is a short series of clips with a comic theme. This film was recognized by Richard Lester and paved the way for him to guide the first Beatles movie: "A Night of Impatience." Richard Lester directed and wrote the music for his first film in 1959. This film was titled Running, Jumping & Standing Still Film, which was designed to be watched only by those who contributed to his production. Because the film was to be seen only by Lester and his partners, a small amount of money and the film cost 70 pounds and was filmed in sepia - movie movie tones in a field on a few Sundays All the photos that have movies were included in the finished production, finite production is eleven minutes in length Running, Jumping & Standing Still Film is a comedy about English Sundays and small hobbies that people do to All events in this movie take place in a field. Some of these comic events include a woman who is cleaning a lawn, a man running around a tree trunk with a needle to play a record, a photographer who develops a film in a pond, an artist assisted in painting with numbers on the face of a model, the man building a tent, an athlete running over the tent and a duel between a man with a knife and a man with a gun. Not only does the movie have fun at the hobbies that people do to pass time, but also have fun with English culture as compared to American culture. Another of the several events in this film includes a group of men and a kite that was built from the UK flag. One of the men jumps inside the kite while the other men try to fly it, and the kite breaks. According to Neil Sinyard, author of Richard Lester's film, this event symbolizes the United Kingdom as less in power and technology than the United States during the space age. According to this scene, British kites fly primitively while Americans, the world power after the Second World War, fly with extremely advanced missiles and spacecraft.
