Making the film as dark and fuzzy as possible to fill budget deficits is a strategy often used in low-budget horror films, but it takes it too far. It's so light and unclear (and it's almost entirely at night to boot) that you often come to a black screen (though maybe the bad video transfer can also do that). Unfortunately, "dark" is also the best word to describe the plot of the film. The directors throw various horror (and non-original) ideas without any logic, and at the middle of the day you will have the feeling that they have abandoned their effort to make a horror movie; you know that when you see characters who should be in mortal danger (or, on some occasions, even death) to make small talk .... (* 1/2)
