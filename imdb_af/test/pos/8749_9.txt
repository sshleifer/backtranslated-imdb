Compared to the well-known Hollywood remake, this movie is less flashy, but much stronger to provide an overall picture of the drug problem. The remake loses the most interesting of the three plot threads, that of a farmer whose scant livelihoods depend on drugs as a paid cash crop and whose increasing involvement in the drug trade in an attempt to improve himself, destroys himself. and his family. In addition, the story of the daughter's addiction goes into trouble, not just of withdrawal, but also of high likelihood of relapse. These original miniseries make good use of the additional time to go into the issues more thoroughly and stay stronger on this controversial subject.
