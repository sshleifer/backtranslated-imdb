The only print of CHIKAMATUS MONOGATARI I could find was uncomfortable - I almost couldn't see it. This is a pity, as it is one of the biggest Mizoguchi movies. The story - which I believe has done before and since then by other Japanese directors - is a bit more straightforward than my favorite Mizoguchi films (SANSHO THE BAILIFF and UGETSU MONOGATARI) and is essentially a story of tragic novels, in this case ' a transgressive romance that crosses strict class boundaries. As always at Mizoguchi, there is an extremely outspoken tone of challenge, and - poor pressure aside - I was very happy. As with all Mizoguchi's movies, I'm eagerly awaiting a restored DVD release - whenever it comes ...
