"Watch the Skies" (2005 - 60 minutes) is an excellent documentary on films of Science Fiction. It was produced and directed by critic Richard Schickel, author of over 20 books on this theme. Mark Hamill is the documentary narrator. Schickel joins directors such as Steven Spielberg, James Cameron, George Lucas and Ridley Scott to travel in space and time to show some of the most memorable science fiction films of the fifties and also some more recent classics. The documentary shows six different approaches: the paranoia of the atomic war; The fantastic trips to the Moon; The mysterious planet Mars; Good and evil aliens; The post-apocalyptic world; and humanity's future. It offers comments and scenes of the following classic: The Flying Saucers, Rocketship XM, Destination Moon, The Space Children, The Beast of 20,000 Fathoms, Godzilla: King of the Monsters, The Incredibly Shrinking Man, Attack of the Killer Tomatoes, a Travel to the moon, the evil red planet, forbidden planet, the thing of another world, the earth against the flying dishes, I have married a monster of outer space, the invasion of the body-cutters, the blob, the war from the world, the day The Earth still has, ET: The Extra Terrestrial, The Omega Man, The Planet of the Apes, The Terminator, 2001: A Space Odyssey, Things Coming With Metropolis. Highly recommended to Science Fiction fans!
