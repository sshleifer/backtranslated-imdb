In the periphery of São Paulo, the very low middle-class dysfunctional and hypocritical family of Teodoro (Giulio Lopes), Cláudia (Leona Cavalli) and teenager Soninha (Silvia Lourenço) have deep secrets. The religious Teodoro is indeed a hit that is hired to kill people in the area with his friend Waldomiro (Ailton Graça). He has a lover, the very dedicated wife Terezinha (Martha Meola), and he wants to regenerate with her to the country. Cláudia has a young lover, Júlio (Ismael de Araújo), who delivers meat to his father's butcher shop. Soninha is a common sixteen-year-old teenager from the periphery, has active sexual life, smoke grass and likes heavy metal. When Julio is killed and castrated in their environment, the lives of family members change. <br /> <br /> "Contra Todos" is a good low-budget Brazilian movie that lives in the periphery of a large Brazilian city. The story is really, using the common elements of the poor area of ​​the big Brazilian cities (drug dealers, men, fanatical religious evangelical people, hopeless teenagers, etc.), have lots of plot points and a surprising end, and the characters have excellent performances, which act very naturally and make the story completely credible. The camera follows the characters and gives a great dynamic to the movie. In the extras of the DVD, director Roberto Moreira explains that his script had no lines, only the description of the situations, and was only partially released one week before the start of the shooting. The actors trained in workshops and they used a lot of improvisation, the reason for such natural acting. My voice is eight. <br /> <br /> Title (Brazil): "Contra Todos" ("Against Everybody")
