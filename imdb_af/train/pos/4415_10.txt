Although some call it a "Cuban Cinema Paradiso", the movie is closer to a How Green was my valley, a memory movie that mourned for a lost innocence. The film cleverly avoids a political snare to take its sides (pro-Castro? Anti-Castro?), Rather focus on the human weakness of the characters and the importance of the family. Filled with good acting, especially from Mexican actress Diana Bracho, who plays Keitel's wife. A masterpiece, filled with references to classic movies, from CASABLANCA to Chaplin's CITY LIGHTS. Gael Garcia Bernal plays a small role essential for the dramatic delivery of the story.
