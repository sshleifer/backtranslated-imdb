Many of the judges pointed out that Pinjar is in contrast to Bollywood film production. While true, Bollywood films are generally targeted at a particular audience and should be appreciated for achieving their goals in this regard. However, Pinjar is an excellent movie for those who want a change of normal. comparison based Bollywood movie. During the time of Partition between India and Pakistan, Pinjar focuses on a Punjabi girl who is the victim of community and cultural attitudes towards the treatment of women in her time. Paro, the protagonist, is forced to choose between a life with a man who kidnapped her and the refugees hope for a life with her family back in Indian ruling Punjab. More than a matter of Hindus and Muslims, Pinjar identifies a woman's role as a daughter, as a woman and as a mother in India and Pakistan in 1947. Unlike typical Bollywood movies that escape from nature, Pinjar a movie that makes his audience consider these issues during and after the film.
