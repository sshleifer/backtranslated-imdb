As many people know, Mexican theater was very poor after the so-called Golden Age of Mexican Cinema, fortunately in the late 90s and early 21st centuries, movies like La Ley de Herod, Bajo California, Amores Perros, Y Tu Mamá Tambien and Of course El Coronel No Tiene Quien le Escriba appears. El Coronel ... is a wonderful movie that recalls the classic story of Gabriel García Márquez, eliminating the magical realism elements and replacing them with the coarse reality that lived in Mexico, not just by people like the Colonel. waiting for their pensions, but by more than half of the Mexican population, living in full poverty. The film's characters represent satirical classical characters that occur in Mexican society, such as the nationalist colonel, the cold and even the ambitious priest, the hypocrite, but at the same time loyal companion, the tolerant and patient woman, the hidden homosexuals. movie, is a must-see if you want to know more about Mexican society, and especially if you want to see a beautiful movie, through one of Mexico's best directors
