The only imprint CHIKAMATUS MONOGATARI, which I managed to find, was terrible - I almost could not watch it. Which is a shame, as it is among the biggest films of Mizoguchi. The story, which I believe was made before and since then by other Japanese directors, is a bit more straightforward than my favorite Mizoguchi films (SANSHO THE BAILIFF and UGETSU MONOGATARI), and is essentially a story of a tragic novel, in this case a transgressive of the novel it crosses the strict boundaries of the classes. As always in the case of Mizoguchi, the tone is defiantly elegant, and, apart from a bad print, I was very pleased. As with all Mizoguchi films, I look forward to the release of the restored DVD - whenever that happens ...
