Right from the brilliant mind of the pioneer of animation Vladislav Starevich, “Fétiche” or “Talisman”, as it is called at the present time, stands as a masterpiece of animation, which, unfortunately, is almost forgotten. Nevertheless, the work of this person deserves to be seen, and, in fact, you need to see to be believed, because the outstanding way of animation is incredible. <br /> <br /> Decades before the Toy Story, Starevich conceived of the idea of ​​moving toys in Fétiche, he tells the story of a small stuffed dog that makes friends with the sick daughter of its creator. One day, his creator takes him to sell, and the adventure begins when he tries to find his way home. In his Odyssey, he will go from Paris to hell and find other toys that should have been sold with him. <br /> <br /> Watching this movie is a surreal experience, as Starevich makes every imaginable creature comes alive with grace and beauty. Other toys include a beautiful ballerina who loves a thief, but she is also secretly loved by a clown, forming a love triangle; an old woman, a stuffed cat and a stuffed monkey complete the group. Each toy is so detailed and very expressive that one can understand their motives without words. <br /> <br /> Indeed, the surrealistic atmosphere of the whole animation is wonderful. It is hard to believe that work of this magnitude was made in 1934, because it looks even better than most modern animations. The influence that he has on modern animators, such as Tim Burton and Henry Selick, is very significant. <br /> <br /> This short masterpiece is a masterpiece of animation and an outstanding work of a single genius who did everything himself and who influenced animators for decades. The works of Starevich are an immortal work of art that everyone should see. This work is intended not only for children, but also for adults, it will like it and will probably catch most of the subtext hidden in the film. <br /> <br /> It can be found on the DVD "Vampyr" as a bonus.
