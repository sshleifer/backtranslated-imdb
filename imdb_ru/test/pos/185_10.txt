If you think that any war movie can make you really feel the war, you need to watch Cranes Fly (in English, Cranes Fly). It tells the story of Veronica (Tatiana Samoilova) and Boris (Alexey Batalov), in love on the eve of World War II. They walk along the embankment, watch the cranes fly by, when war begins. Boris quickly goes to war. Veronica hides with her family and eventually marries a son whom she does not love. Boris, meanwhile, continues to run through the countryside, battling with the Nazis and reliving all the horrors of war, until he runs out of energy. When Veronica, who works in a military hospital, receives this news, she refuses to accept it until Boris's body arrives home on one of the trains. At the same time, the radio reports that Germany surrendered, and the Allied powers won the war; The Soviet Union lost 27 million citizens, but this is the beginning of a new era. <br /> <br /> This film did an excellent job, showing the impact of war on a person, not only on the battlefield, but also on how it affected. civilian population. This is definitely a movie that everyone should see.
