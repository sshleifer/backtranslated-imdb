When character Liv Ulman says: “I feel like I’m in someone else’s dream, and they’ll feel ashamed when they wake up,” she means not only the reluctance to play public war games, she means being ignorant of life. At the end of the film, when she says that she had a dream about having a child, and she tried to take care of it, but she forgot something else, it means that she forgot what she learned in the war, she just survived that, like her own mother before her, she would not be able to pass on any vital lessons to her own child. And, therefore, the cycle of the shame of ignorance will continue ... to infinity ...
