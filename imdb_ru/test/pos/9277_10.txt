“Watch the Sky” (2005 - 60 minutes) is a great documentary about science fiction films. It was prepared and directed by critic Richard Schickel, the author of more than 20 books on this topic. Mark Hamill is a documentary narrator. Schickel joins directors such as Steven Spielberg, James Cameron, George Lucas and Ridley Scott to travel through time and space to show some of the most memorable science fiction films of the fifties, as well as some later classic works. The documentary shows six different approaches: paranoia of atomic warfare; Fantastic trips to the moon; Mysterious planet Mars; Good and evil aliens; Post-apocalyptic world; and the future of humanity. It presents comments and scenes from the following classic works: Flying Saucer, Rocket Ship, Destination Moon, Space Children, Beast with 20,000 Fathoms, Godzilla: Monster King, Incredible Shrinking Man "," Attack of the killers of tomatoes "," Trip "to the Moon, Angry Red Planet, Forbidden Planet, Things from another world, Earth against flying saucers, I married a monster from space, Invasion of hijackers, Drop, War of the Worlds, Day Earth has stopped, ET: alien, Omega Man, Planet of the Apes, Ter Minator, 2001: A Space Odyssey, Upcoming and Metropolis. Highly recommended to science fiction fans!
