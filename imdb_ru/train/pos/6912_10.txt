An excellent comic farce from Paul Mazursky, Richard Dreyfus, plays Jack Noah a fairly successful actor who makes a film on the spot in the fictional Latin American banana republic Parador, ruled by fascist Alphonse Sim, who unfortunately suffered a heart attack by indulging in too many local cocktails! Raul Julia plays a fat police chief who makes a reluctant Noah impersonate the Just dead dictator, whom Noah bears a remarkable resemblance, Sonya Braga plays the glamorous lustful lover of a dictator who gives Noah several lessons about how to act as a dictator Jonathan Winter literally completes acting in the role of the CIA in Parador, posing as a hammock merchant. Can Noah defeat the people of Parador? and keep the rebels? And give an idea for life, without losing it in the process? Sammy Davis Jr. has a cameo who funnyly sings Parador anthem, as well as Begin the Beguine, “Number One Frog” (Fernando Rey appears as a good servant, Charo is also on hand, like a busty maid, Maurice Jarre, great.
