Rachel Griffiths writes and directs this award-winning short film. A heart story about how to deal with grief and cherish the memory of those we loved and lost. Although in just 15 minutes, Griffiths manages to capture so much emotion and truth in a film in a short time. Bad Thingwell gives a touching insight into how Will, a widower, struggling to cope with the death of his wife. The will confronts the harsh reality of loneliness and helplessness when he begins to take care of his beloved cow Ruth, Tulip. The film shows the grief and responsibility of those they loved and lost. Good cinematography, excellent direction and great acting. It will bring tears to all those who have lost a loved one and survived.
