Trudi Linz (Rene Rousseau) was one very happy woman many years ago. She was the wife of a rich doctor and had a lot of extracurricular money. Her passion was animals, and she devoted herself to providing shelter for furry on her land plots. Trudy also raised two chimps in her home to be more like children. They were dressed in clothes and had many comforts. One day she finds out about an abandoned gorilla. Knowing nothing about big monkeys, she relies on her husband’s medical skills and expert advice to save the gorilla’s life. Out of danger, Trudy decides that she will raise a gorilla, as well as one of her children. It has worked well for years, and Buddy, the gorilla, is a truly amazingly smart addition to her home. But Buddy is also a gorilla, and his strength and curiosity become enormous. Can Trudy keep Buddy under control? For those who love animals, Buddy is a must-see film. Based on a true story, Trudy and her monkey develop relationships that are unique in the annals of animal history and knowledge. Of course, in the film, Buddy is not a real gorilla, but a mechanical one, but he is very close to appearing absolutely real. Rousseau gives a good game, like a lady, ahead of her time, and supporting players are also pretty good. The costumes are exemplary, as they correspond to the early epoch of history, and the settings and production values ​​remain outstanding. But, most importantly, animals are abundant here, not only buddy, but also charming chimpanzees, ducks, rabbits and so on. For those who want to watch a movie and travel to the heavens of animals, here on earth is a great selection of films. All animal lovers, and even those who just want to see a wonderful family movie, will fight with Buddy.
