On the periphery of São Paulo, the very low middle class of the dysfunctional and hypocritical family of Theodoro (Giulio Lopez), Claudia (Leon Cavalli) and the teenager Sonigny (Sylvia Lorenzo) have deep secrets. Religious Theodoro is truly a hired killer hired to kill people next door to his friend Valdomiro (Ailton Graça). He has a mistress, a very pious woman Terezinya (Martha Meola), and he wants to be reborn, going with her to the country. Claudia has a young lover Julio (Ismael de Araoujo), who supplies meat for his father’s butcher shop. Soninja is a typical sixteen-year-old teenager on the periphery, leading an active sex life, smoking weed and loving heavy metal. When Julio is killed and castrated in the neighborhood, the lives of family members change. <br /> <br /> “Contra Todos” is a great low-budget Brazilian film that tells about life on the periphery of a big Brazilian. city. This story is very real, uses the usual elements of a poor area of ​​large Brazilian cities (drug dealers, hired killers, fanatical religious evangelists, hopeless teenagers, etc.), has many plot points and an unexpected end, and the characters have excellent performances, very natural behavior and absolutely believable story. The camera follows the characters, giving the film more dynamics. In “Extras of the DVD,” director Roberto Moreira explains that there were no lines in his script, only a description of the situations, and it was partially revealed only a week before the shooting began.
