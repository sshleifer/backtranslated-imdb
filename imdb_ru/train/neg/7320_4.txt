Slavery, humiliation, S & M shows and more. The plot is flat, in fact, just a banal installation for stylishly depraved scenery. The host of the aforementioned show, a stupid little man, spewing fornication, swaying around the stage in dresses, was almost as distressingly distracting as attempts at clever editing. A dreamlike end was felt. To the credit of the film, though, Aya Sugimoto was quite convincing as a tortured leader. The Flower and the Snake were compared with eyes wide shut, but besides some minor surface similarities, Kubrick is easily a more multi-layered, artistic, and atmospheric picture.
