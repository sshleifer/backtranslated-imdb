Guy de Maupassant was a novelist who wrote a novel about a man, a poor man, without any moral qualities. He just wanted to succeed in a society where all people, politicians, businessmen, journalists, women are corrupt. The only king is MONEY. The hero of Maupassant, Charles Forestier, is becoming higher and higher in the scale of society thanks to his task of seduction. He is in love with all the women who could help him in his actions to climb the steps of society. At the end of the novel, he married the daughter of the largest owner of a daily newspaper in the largest church in Paris: La Madeleine. "Le Tout Paris" there. He has a fortune and more, he will become a member of parliament, and then a minister. "Useless" women are out of his sight, but he always keeps in touch with beautiful and useful women. The painting "PRIVATE ACTIONS BEL AMI" is the history of MORALITY. This is all, but not a story in the idea of ​​Maupassant. Why did they put "BEL AMI" in the title?
