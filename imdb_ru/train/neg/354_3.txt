Look closely at the wonderful collection of talents brought together to create this film: Connery, Fishburn, Capshaw, Harris, Underwood, Beatty, Tigpen, even Sleazak, Cameos, and Plympton. They argue, in spikes, the adage that a good cast cannot save a bad script. The storyline requires so many leaps of faith from the audience that its improbability should have surpassed even Hollywood standards. This is not particularly original, and the "turns" are absolutely cruel.
