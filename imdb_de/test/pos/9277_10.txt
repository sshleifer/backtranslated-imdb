"Watch the Skies" (2005 - 60 minutes) is an excellent documentary about science fiction films. Produced and directed by critic Richard Schickel, author of more than 20 books on the subject. Mark Hamill is the documentary narrator. Schickel joins the directors as Steven Spielberg, James Cameron, George Lucas, and Ridley Scott to take a time and space journey to showcase some of the fifties's most memorable science fiction films as well as some recent classics. The documentary shows six different approaches: the paranoia of the nuclear war; The fantastic trips to the moon; The enigmatic planet Mars; Good and bad aliens; The post-apocalyptic world; and the future of humanity. It presents comments and scenes on the following classics: The Flying Saucers, Rocketship XM, Destination Moon, The Space Children, The Beast of 20,000 Fathoms, Godzilla: King of the Monsters, The Incredible Shrinkman, Attack of the Killer Tomatoes, A Journey to the Moon, angry red planet, the forbidden planet, the thing from another world, earth against the flying saucers, I married a monster from space, the invasion of the body-eaters, the blob, the war of the worlds, the day The earth stood still, ET : The Alien, The Omega Man, The Planet of the Apes, The Terminator, 2001: A Space Odyssey, Future Things and Metropolis. Highly recommended for science fiction fans!
