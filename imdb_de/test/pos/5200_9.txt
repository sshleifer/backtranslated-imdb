Damn wonderful. Recommended by a friend who knew that I liked Thomas Kretschmann in The Pianist and Downfall. I loved the flow of the narrative - how the characters moved through shock, fear, disintegration, despair, and total annihilation of hope and ideals of valor. A first-class anti-war movie that must have caused a sensation in Germany. No proud moment for anyone and the study of a generation lost. This film was excellent in conveying the removal of command from the ground forces and beating the utter futility of trying to control the untamed nature and the Russian psyche. If I had not known, I would have thought that this is a Russian movie, it would be so fatalistic. What an uncompromising ending. A pieta
