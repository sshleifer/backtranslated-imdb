The Patriot is a well thought out, well produced film that will draw the viewer directly into the story and include it to the very end. Granted, it's not in the same direction as Under Siege, et. al., but this is definitely the brand movie of Seagal's second epoch of films. Not so much action movies that remember a story thrown in for good reason, but a good story that matches a certain point and is linked to some of Seagal's inimitable action sequences. Is it the kind of movie that will excite hardcore action junkies? Probably not. But for those of us who not only like our action but also appreciate a well told story, this is a big step forward for Seagal. It's almost like the transformation that Clint Eastwood made as he tried to emerge under the shadow of his "Dirty Harry" series. If he had progressed in the direction in which he had moved, he would have been permanently enshrined in a certain role and would have guaranteed the brevity of his career ...
