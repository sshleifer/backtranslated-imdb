A good but not great movie, The Great Dictator, is Chaplin's first talkie. It is a good effort and sometimes a remarkable satire on Hitler, but unfortunately the moments of Chaplin brilliance offered here are abandoned by the tendency to use too much unnecessary dialogue. Yes, Chaplin, the silent screen-master, simply uses too many words in this film, and his tendency to write long speeches and redundant dialogue passages would be reflected in his other talkie films as (better) "limelight." Nevertheless, Chaplin himself was extremely daring in the creation of this film and his performance in double roles is certainly memorable.
