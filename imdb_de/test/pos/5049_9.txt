To qualify my use of "realistic" in the summary, not many of the old people I know seem to be famous maestros, blind people, etc. - and I've never been older either. These little issues, the relationships between the characters in this film and the emotions expressed in them, were completely realistic and straightforward. Although I am not yet 30, I have seen many features of my relationship with my wife in the relationship between the main character and his wife. For those who do not die young (there's a great line in the movie about when the two best friends talk about dying boys and one of them says - and I paraphrase, we missed our chance - we just have to hold it) ), we will all be where those characters are someday. I know that many moviegoers would prefer to be torn away from reality instead of being confronted with it, but even they could enjoy the sweet memory of our mortality - and fully appreciate the meaning of life - that this movie is ,
