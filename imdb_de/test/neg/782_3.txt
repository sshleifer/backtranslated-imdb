The Box is a movie with great potential, but the creators have totally misused this potential. The film seemed to last forever because of the boring family dinners and scenes about school and job dialogues between actions. These scenes could and must be deleted in my opinion, to maintain the excitement and the thrill. The philosophy of the free will of man has potential and seems to refer to the philosophy of Thomas Hobbes (1588-1679), but we find ourselves regretfully met with magic and nosebleeds, even Harry Potter would fail with his class! Probably the best part was the moment when Norma Lewis (Cameron Diaz) was shot by her loving and caring husband as an act of human free will. I wonder how Hobbes would react if he could ...
