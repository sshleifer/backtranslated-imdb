First of all, I met the director as he went with a historical film camera to second world war reenactments and made videos of the events that looked damned good. I really wish he had kept that. For as much as I applauded what he did with a "nearly-to-nothing" budget when I jumped out of the DVD, I was just wondering why I had not eaten anything for nearly two hours. There is no real conspiracy, you do not care what happens to the characters (maybe the Italian troops and some of the Germans), and the end is another "art film" commentary on the futility of war. I could have told somebody how it would end as soon as I got through the first ten minutes. I knew that the Germans would have some heroic volunteers who would fight to the death. I knew most Quartmaster GIs would be killed, it was just too damn obvious! And while I was studying the subject, I was shocked that so much of the Axis site was doing well, but the GI site was weird. The entire character development was clear for non-English speaking roles. The GIs were just beaten hard in this movie. And I wonder if they even had anyone on the set who understood how the US Army works? The expressions, terminology, and actions were clearly written by someone who had absolutely no knowledge of the American military. If I had known now who made this film, I could have sworn that it was from a German ...
