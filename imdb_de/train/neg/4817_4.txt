Some pictures ("Rosemary's Baby" for example) have achieved this; others (like "Necromancy") do not have this and the potentially scary stuff looks crazy and stupid. This movie has some atmospheric moments, but about half of the dialogue is hard to spot (sometimes poorly recorded, sometimes incomprehensible), and Orson Welles, who gets the best payroll, has a role that does him so well that you have him to assume that he was desperately looking for work. Or maybe he just had fun ... (* 1/2)
