I was expecting a documentary about the tobacco industry in North Carolina. Instead, I watched a man regret the fact that his great-grandfather lost his tobacco empire to the Duke family. And that went on and on. If Mr. McElwee's family had prevailed against the dukes, I doubt Mr. McElwee would have problems with the number of deaths from tobacco disease. I grew up near the area where Mr. McElwee's family started the tobacco business. I've been expecting more than just McEwee's constant focus on his family. I have learned very little about the history of tobacco in the NC economy and its impact on the state economy through a stricter regulation of tobacco. The countless references to the movie "Bright Leaves" are out of place. What if Gary Cooper played Mr. McElwee's great-grandfather? By showing old film clips of a fictionalized film, does the viewer understand the role of tobacco in North Carolina's economy? I did not
