Making the movie as dark and visual as possible to cover up budget deficits is a common strategy in low-budget horror movies, but it goes too far. It's so dimly lit and bleak (and it happens almost exclusively at night) that you often look at a black screen (though maybe the bad video transfer had something to do with it). Unfortunately, "gloomy" is also the best word to describe the plot of the film. The filmmakers make a variety of (and non-original) horror ideas with no pretense of logic, and halfway you have the feeling that they have almost given up trying to make a good horror movie; You know it when you see characters in mortal danger (or even dead in some cases) to talk ...
