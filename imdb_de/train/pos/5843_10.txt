Lubitsch's latest production, but not his least interesting film. Somehow largely ignored by critics, as he could not finish it himself and since the movie was not signed by Preminger, whom he did most of the staging ... A very weird mix of musical (a reminder of The Merry Widow?) and classic Lubitsch touch sentimentalism (an impossible love story like Cluny Brown), but a very smart and intelligent one, not to be understood as the nostalgia of a lost world, but rather a testament to eternal feelings based on the stupidity of humanity and In times of war with a "moral" lesson that remains the same today as in 1948, Billy Wilder is said to have responded to Preminger, who complained about Lubitsch's funerals about having lost a big man, and replied that we still have his films and that summarizes all about this lady in Ermine ...
