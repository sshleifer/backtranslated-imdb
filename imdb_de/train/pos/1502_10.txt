The late, great Robert Bloch (author of PSYCHO, for those of you who were not paying attention) wrote this story of horror and it was absolutely one of the scariest films I've ever seen as a kid. (I had to run MILES just to see a movie, and usually it was dark when I came out of the theater, watching a horror movie was always annoying, but especially when done as well as this one.) The Opportunity to watch videotape a few years ago (which should be a last resort), I was surprised how well it lasted. Do the horror test: look alone at night and then tell me it's not scary ...
