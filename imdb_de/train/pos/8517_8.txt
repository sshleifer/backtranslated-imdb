Russ and Valerie discuss the creation of a family. The couple lives in a posh apartment and runs an auction business that deals with precious collectibles. At the same time, a dedicated adoption agency owner takes a short break and leaves the orphanage to his father (Leslie Nielsen). Father Harry is in the rental business and he has the ingenious idea of ​​"renting" some children from the orphanage to couples like Russ and Valerie. Harry, who becomes aware of the couple's dilemma, offers a family of siblings for a rental period of 10 days! Brandon, Kyle and Molly move into the apartment with their temporary parents, with amusing consequences as the new janitors with children are inexperienced. But where is the possibility of a happy ending? This is a cute family movie. The actors, including Nielsen as a wheeler dealer and Christopher Lloyd as a friendly apartment doorman, are all wonderful. The script is snappy and fun and the overall production values ​​are quite high. Yes, if only life could be like that! Orphans everywhere deserve the chance to prove that they are adorable and that they can bring so much joy to the parents who are considering adoption. If you want to show your family a movie that is based on good values ​​but is also very entertaining, you will find this movie. It is guaranteed that everyone will laugh, even when their hearts are melting.
