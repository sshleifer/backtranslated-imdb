It was very heartwarming. As a Frenchman living abroad, I particularly liked this "TV" film, which in my opinion deserves a wider distribution. The acting was good and the bonds between each member of this rural French family were so strong and so natural. Undoubtedly a great film about the concept of family and inclusion in the face of homosexuality and AIDS. One of the strongest aspects of the film is this privileged relationship between the eldest son (Leo), who has become infected with HIV, and the youngest son (Marcel), to whom the rest of the family tries to hide the situation. The two characters gradually explore each other as the time runs out for Leo, unwilling to spend the rest of his life drugged ...
