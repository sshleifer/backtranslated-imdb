Walt Disney's CINDERELLA tells a story that everyone knows, embellishing it with humor and excitement while retaining the essential charm of the story. Disney's artists give the film an appealing storybook look that exudes a delicious fairytale atmosphere. It's beautiful when it's conventionally animated; The highlight is the captivating scene where the Fairy Godmother transforms a pumpkin into a majestic carriage and Cinderella's rags a beautiful dress. Mack David, Al Hoffman and Jerry Livingston deliver beautiful songs like "A Dream Is A Wish Your Heart Wants" and "Bibbidi-Bobbidi-Boo" that enhance both the scenario and the characters. <br /> <br /> Even though Cinderella's story is predictable, she delivers such an exciting melodrama to share the worries and fears of the titular heroine and her animal friends. Both the evil stepmother and her terrible cat Lucifer pose a formidable threat that threatens the dreams and aspirations of Cinderella and the mice. This threat gives the story a strong conflict that arouses the interest of the audience. The tension of the film, however, is offset by a cheerful sweetness, especially in the musical numbers. These are the areas in which the engaging personalities of Cinderella and her friends thrill the audience into caring for them. Overall, Walt Disney's CINDERELLA is a wonderful family entertainment that has held up remarkably well after half a century.
