This is undoubtedly the best documentary ever produced, providing an accurate and epic account of World War II from the invasion of Poland in 1939 until the end of the war in 1945. Honest and to the point Documentary presents views from both sides of the conflict that give the war a very human face. At the same time, the tactics and importance of the battles are not overlooked, and much work has been done to give a detailed picture of the war and, in particular, the high, low and turning points of the Allies. As a documentary produced in the UK, this 26-part series focuses mainly on Britain, but the contribution of Russia and America is not ignored. This is just one benefit of this series. <br /> <br /> Another noteworthy aspect is the score, the music and the whole feeling of the documentary film is characterized by turmoil, struggle and perseverance. Like a movie, this series leaves the viewer in no doubt about the distress of the Allies and Germans during the war, the height of which builds at the end of each episode to a climax that serves to curtail the rudeness of the Second World War. After considering all 26 years, the viewer has a thorough knowledge of the war and is amazed at how much we owe to the members of the previous generation.
