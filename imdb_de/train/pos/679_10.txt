This supernatural Peter Weir thriller is truly one of the most immersive and intriguing films ever seen. Richard Chamberlain is doing his best as an Australian lawyer defending a group of young Aboriginal people accused of murder. As he gets closer to the case, he discovers more about the main culprit Chris, and not least about himself. Chris tells him that he is a Mulkurul, which seems to be a race of supernatural beings who lived in Australia thousands of years ago , At the same time, an exceptionally high rainfall seems to confirm the Aboriginal prophecy about the coming of the LAST WAVE, which will drown the world. <br /> <br /> The dream sequences and the supernatural effects contribute to this film It is a spectacular experience. Olivia Hamnett and David Gulpilil are solid in supporting roles as well as the guy with the tricky name who plays Charlie, the old Aborigine who can turn into an owl. The climax and the end do not disappoint, unlike many other supernatural thrillers that fall flat after a promising hour. However, this can not be called a pure thriller. It is also a drama and talks about spirituality and spiritual identity in the modern world. A masterful work by Peter Weir, the master of visually stunning dramas.
