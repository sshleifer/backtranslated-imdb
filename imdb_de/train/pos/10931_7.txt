There is no romance or other subplot to this film, it's action and intrigue, which makes it a real kung fu movie. An aging master sends his last disciple Yan Tieh to stop his five former disciples, whose styles represent five venomous animals centipede, snake, scorpion, lizard and toad. Despite the word "Venom" in the title, none of these students use poisons to kill their opponents. Yan Tieh said of his teacher that he is no match for the five former students. He has to find one with whom he can band together to defeat the other four. How Yan Tieh and the others find each other is the intrigue in the story, with good kung-fu action that spreads throughout the story. <br /> <br /> Recognized as a cult classic, this film has already starred in kung fu action movies. It is well known that other films refer to the five styles depicted in this story. <br /> <br /> It's not an artistic masterpiece, with the usual bad syncs and stupid actors, but the movie is one of the best of its kind because it focuses on all the ingredients of the kung fu action movie of its time give an extra concentrated dose of it. <br /> <br /> A movie you have to see if you are a kung fu movie fan.
