Many of the reviewers have made it their mission to realize that Pinjar is different from the mill films produced in Bollywood. While Bollywood films are generally targeted to a specific audience, they should be commended for achieving their goals in this regard. <br /> <br /> However, Pinjar is a great movie for anyone looking for a change from the normal Bollywood film based on equations. Pinjar plays at the time of the split between India and Pakistan and focuses on a Punjabi girl who is a victim of social and cultural attitudes towards treating women in her time. Paro, the protagonist, has to decide between living with a man who abducted her, and the fleeting hope of living with her family in Punjab, India. Pinjar is more than an edition of Hindus and Muslims and speaks and defines the role of a woman as a daughter, wife and mother in India and Pakistan in 1947. Unlike typical Bollywood films, which are fleeting in nature, Pinjar is one Film lets its audience look at these topics during and after the movie.
