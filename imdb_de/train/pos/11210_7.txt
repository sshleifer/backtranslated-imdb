Trudy Lintz (Rene Russo) was a very happy woman many years ago. She was the wife of a wealthy doctor and had a lot of extracurricular money. Her passion was animals, and she was dedicated to protecting the furry animals on her property. Trudy also attracted two chimpanzees in her home to make her look like children. They dressed and had many amenities. One day she finds out about an abandoned gorilla. Unaware of the great apes, she relies on her husband's medical skills and expert advice to save the gorilla's life. Once out of danger, Trudy decides that she will raise the gorilla as one of her children. This has worked well for years and Buddy, the gorilla, is a truly remarkable smart addition to her home. But Buddy is also a gorilla and his strength and curiosity are enormous. Will Trudy be able to keep Buddy under control? For those who love animals, Buddy is a must-have movie. Based on a true story, Trudy and her monkey develop a relationship that is unique in the annals of animal history and history. Of course Buddy is not a real gorilla in the movie, but a mechanical gorilla, but he seems to be very real. Russo shows a nice performance as a lady before her time and the supportive players are also pretty nice. The costumes are exemplary, as befits the earlier epoch of history, and the settings and production values ​​are superb. Most importantly, animals abound here, not just buddy, but also the adorable chimpanzees, ducks, rabbits and so on. For those who want to watch a movie and be transported to the animal sky, here on earth, this is a great movie selection. All animal lovers and even those who just want to see a great family movie, will go through Buddy "monkeys".
