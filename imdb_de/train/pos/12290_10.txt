The Hotel Du Nord is a gripping guilt drama in which Marcel Carne portrayed an entertaining story of unfortunate love that also acts as a rebellion against the cruel world. The film is based exclusively on a pair of hapless lovers. Pierre and Renee were wrong They believed that suicide would put an end to their misery. The Hotel Du Nord has its own inimitable charm, as its inhabitants have become an integral part of the establishment. There is a sense of togetherness as they all flock to the Hotel Du Nord for food, chat, etc.Marcel Carne has remained true to the spirit of the films produced in the 1930s and 1940s, as the Hotel Du Nord has a kind of nostalgic mood , Carne, while reviving the life of Parisian streets, was able to create a sort of nostalgia for black and black. White gives his work a unique genre of poetic realism. The Hotel Du Nord can be described as the quintessence of cinematographic populism. The 14th July ball scene on the banks of the Saint-Martin Canal is still a great sequence. Mense's popularity is reflected in the fact that the Hotel Du Nord has been declared a National Monument.
