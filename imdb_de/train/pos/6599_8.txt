In the outskirts of São Paulo, the dysfunctional and hypocritical family of the very low middle classes of Teodoro (Giulio Lopes), Cláudia (Leona Cavalli) and the teenager Soninha (Sílvia Lourenço) have deep secrets. The religious Teodoro is indeed a hit man who wants to kill people in the neighborhood with his friend Waldomiro (Ailton Graça). He has a lover, the very pious Mrs. Terezinha (Martha Meola), and he wants to regenerate and go with her to the countryside. Cláudia has a young lover, Júlio (Ismael de Araújo), who supplies meat for his father's butcher shop. Soninha is a 16-year-old teenager in the periphery. He has an active sex life, smokes grass and loves heavy metal. When Júlio is killed and neutered in his neighborhood, the lives of family members change. <br /> <br /> "Contra Todos" is a great low-budget Brazilian movie that depicts life in the periphery of a great Brazilian city. The story is very real, uses the usual elements of the poor area of ​​the big Brazilian cities (drug dealers, batsmen, fanatical religious evangelists, hopeless teenagers, etc.), has many plot points and a surprising ending and the characters excel, which look very natural and the Make history absolutely believable. The camera follows the characters and gives the film a great dynamic. In the extras of the DVD, the director Roberto Moreira explains that his screenplay had no lines, just the description of the situations, and was released in part only a week before the start of filming. The actors have been training in workshops and they have used much improvisation, which is the reason for such natural action.
