Wow, what a great roll! Julia Roberts, John Cusack, Christopher Walken, Catherine Zeta-Jones, Hank Azaria ... What is it? A script, you say? Now you're just greedy! Surely such a charismatic bunch of thespians will weave such fetching tapestries of cinematic wonder that a script will be unnecessary? You think so, but no. America's Sweethearts is a missed chance after another. It's like everyone involved woke up before every day's writing / shooting / editing, and though "you know what? I've worked pretty hard lately, and it's guaranteed to be a hit with all those big names, right? I'm just Going with a cruise together and letting someone else carry the can. "So much potential, yet so painful to sit through. There is not a single aspect of this thing that does not suck. Even Julia's fat suit is lame.
