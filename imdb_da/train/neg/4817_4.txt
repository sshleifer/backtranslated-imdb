When you make a thriller about witchcraft, I think you have to do everything you can to help the audience suspend their unbelief for the movie to work. Some images ("Rosemary's Baby", for example) have achieved this; Others (like "Necromancy") do not have, and the potentially scary material comes across as corny and goofy. This movie has some atmospheric moments, but about half of the dialogue is hard to compose (sometimes it is poorly recorded, at other times just incomprehensible) and Orson Welles, who gets top billing, has a role so under him that you have to assume he was desperate for the job. Or maybe he simply had fun ..... (* 1/2)
