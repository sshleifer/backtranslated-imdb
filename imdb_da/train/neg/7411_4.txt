An interesting idea that involves an alternative dimension within a hospital. It reminded - Stephen King's "Langoliers", "Kingdom Hospital" and old twilight zone episodes. Atmospherically strong, setup was great. There were also some very clever 'time loop' moments that always have the melting point appeal. The story had lots of vague references that made me believe that all the subsequent disgrace of - time slips / ghosts / a weird velvet blown demon and a rather silly heavy metal ghoul thing would be explained. And it was dark floors seriously let me down. Based on my view, the film gives NO EXPLANATION to what lies behind the events. While some ambiguity is always expected / welcomed in these kinds of movies. Dark floors took it to new heights of vigilance. I don't expect things to be wrapped up in a beautiful ribbon, but I don't have to feel "Huh? .. is that it?" Should I dose? Maybe I made a mistake in chapter crossing? "Dark Floors left me with a deeply unsatisfactory suspicion that" it was all a dream, "which is a shame, because until the last wheel I was aboard and enjoyed the movie.
