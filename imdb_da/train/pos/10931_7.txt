There is no romance or other side plot for this movie, it's action and intrigue all the way, making it a real man's kung-fu movie. <br /> <br /> An elderly master sends his last disciple Yan Tieh to stop his five former students whose styles represent five poisonous animal daisies, snake, scorpion, lizard and toad. In spite of the word "Venom" in the title, none of these students use poison to kill their opponents. Yan Tieh told by his teacher that he is not a match for the five former students, must find one he can form an alliance with defeating the other four. How Yan Tieh and the others find each other is the plot of the story, with good kung-fu action spread throughout the story. Recognized as a cult classic, this film has already established itself in the annals of Kung Fu movies. It is well known that other films refer to the five styles depicted in this story. It is not an artistic masterpiece, with the usual bad dubbing and corny acting, but the film is one of the best of that kind, because it focused on all the ingredients of kung-fu action movies from time to time and provides an extra concentrated dose of them. <br /> <br /> A movie to see if you are a kungfu movie fan.
