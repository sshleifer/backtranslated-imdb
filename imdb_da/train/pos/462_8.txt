"Luzhin Defense" is at the forefront a story of an idiosyncratic chess savior (Turturro) being consumed by the game, and in the background through flashbacks his boyhood and the forces that created the man are his. Factor in good and evil in the form of his love interest (Watson) and former "mentor" (Wilson) respectively, and you have a clapping drama twisted with exciting and delicate humorous moments blended with the rich scenic beauty of Italy's Lake Como and the intensity of high chess game. An excellent movie for those in period dramas, authors euphemistic chess game metaphor for orgasmic joy is an indication of the subtle humor behind the movie.
