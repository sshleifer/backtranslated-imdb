The 40-year-old virgin (2005) **** Steve Carell, Catherine Keener, Paul Rudd, Romany Malco, Seth Rogen, Elizabeth Banks, Leslie Mann, Jane Lynch, Gerry Bednob, Shelley Malil, Kat Dennings. Hysterical fun high concept comedy about the title Andy Stitzer (wonderfully played by perennial second banana Carell in a truly unusual comic breakthrough performance sure to stratosphere him to the A list), a tech service rep for an electronics store in Southern California that is found out of his secretive identity by a trio of well-meaning, yet clueless womanizing associates (Rudd, Malco & Rogen, each degree more fun than the next) decided to get their friend deflowered regardless of the cost. What follows is an unlikely yet very warm-hearted romance with a lively mother (the amazing Keener has a lot of fun here) leading to adding more fuel to the Andy fire. A surprisingly good-spirited and unapologetically raunchy romantic comedy; the funniest side "There is something about Mary" with a sharp observant script by director Judd Apatow and Carell that contain some amazing gut-busting sequences, including a scathingly accurate David Caruso joke, homophobic debunking ribbing, send-ups of "date- a-paloozas 'and demystify the wives' war with naughty aplomb, a true winner and an instant classic;
