The only print of the CHIKAMATUS MONOGATARI I could find was scary - I could hardly see it. It's a shame as this is among the greatest Mizoguchi movies. The story - which I think had happened before and after by other Japanese directors - is a bit more straightforward than my favorite Mizoguchi films (SANSHO THE BAILIFF and UGETSU MONOGATARI), and is essentially a story of tragic romance, in this case a transgressive romance that crosses strict class boundaries. As always with Mizoguchi, there is an exquisitely expressed tone of trait, and - poor print aside - I was very pleased. As with any Mizoguchi movie, I am eagerly awaiting a restored DVD release - when it comes to ...
