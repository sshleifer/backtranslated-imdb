Watching The Wagonmaster is unlikely to result in deep thoughts, unlike many other amazing Ford movies, such as The Searchers, My Darling Clementine, The Man Who Shot Liberty Valance and Wrath's Grapes, but it will probably produce a sense of awe and deepness. satisfaction. The story is very simple: two cowboys decide to help a wagon take off Mormon's coming to California. Along the way, they run into a medical man whose mule runs away, a group of bank robbers and some Navajos. There is a lot of adventure and excitement on the track, and the movie is full of fun and beauty. The music is absolutely beautiful. The landscape, again from Monument Valley, is as beautiful as it ever was. Plus, how can you go wrong with James Arness? Wagonmaster may not be one of John Ford's better-known films, but it is nevertheless a must-see if you get the chance. 10.9.
