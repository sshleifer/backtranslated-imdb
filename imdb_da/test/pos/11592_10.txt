This story of a man's 28-year struggle for a death that would free him from his already dead body becomes a masterpiece to remember, thanks to a team of artists in a state of grace. Directed, written, edited and scored by Alejandro Amenabar, it touches you from the very first pictures and does not leave your eyes and your heart resting until the last credits thanks to Alejandro and a group of wonderful actors and actresses at their best. Bardem is a play: One of the few comedians who can make a masterpiece from almost any character, the entertaining actresses play a major role in their roles, and the story is told with such a feeling that you laugh and cry in the same minute as we used to do with the great old masterpieces. This year's best film in all senses. 10.10
