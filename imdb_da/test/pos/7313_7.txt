Norma Shearer blinds as she is transformed from a fractured, addle-brained house-wife to an attractive potential divorce. Most of the 1930's have a creaking edge to them - the camera's work is pretty slow at times - but we have to forgive these all-encompassing pleasures for their trump-a-long "qualities". As a guest of the eccentric monastery church Marie Dressler - Shearer interferes with an odd selection of lovelorn types, including her long-lived husband. The dialogue is fun, often times smart and the performances on cue. Shearer and Dressler shine most. Shearer even shows his piano expertise in a musical brief. Her strange but appealing attitude and "affair with the camera" is evident all the time - and she hits all emotional notes, real and on purpose. For his time. . a good show
