Boogie Nights follows a theme that is highly known for gangster movies (although it does not fit into that genre) - the rise and fall. We see the emergence of several individuals, some of them from complete ambiguity, to attain great heights ... and then fall from grace because of their transgressions. I think this is the first move by author / director Paul Thomas Anderson, and it's a great start! I saw elements of other directors' influence, like Robert Altman, but the film has its own originality and plot development. <br /> <br /> Character development is the movie's finest feature. I really identified myself with all the signs and felt their pain and their success with them. All performances were brilliant. (It was especially good to see a small part done by the married veteran, Nina Hartley). Basically, this movie combines comedy and tragedy with the result as one of the best films from 1997, which was snubbed by the Oscars (probably due to "racy" - as they would say - topic and the Academy's general conservatism.
