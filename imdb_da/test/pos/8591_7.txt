An excellent, meticulously produced historical drama - well played, artfully written, shot in ways that convey accurate visual images of Kongo and with more than a few moving moments, especially for those who care about the history of Africa and imperialism. However, a reasonable amount of valuable content is lost in translation, and because names, acronyms and so on are difficult to follow. so i strongly recommend checking a neutral source like wikipedia to get a basic sense of the story depicted (and the subsequent story) before enjoying the movie. If you have the DVD version, there are also some useful historical backgrounds. There is a point at the end of the movie where the name of a character who then speaks to an American accent is actually offered - a simple google search on "lumumba movie censor" or something similar will reveal a truly fascinating (and perhaps disruptive) twist regarding the production of this important film. This film, along with a little outside research, helps contextualize dozens of other films related to Central / East Africa and / or imperialism, for example. hotel rwanda, pushing the hands of the devil, various adjustments of the heart of the conrads, and even "ali" when mohammed ali visits kinshasa.
