Hugh (Ed Harris) is a single senator, determined to run for president. One day, he happens with an old high school classmate named Aggie. Aggie (Diane Keaton) is a prominent and award-winning author with a beautiful face and an independent spirit. Hugh is in love. He convinces Agi to become his fiancée. But will Aggie have to sacrifice the principles of honesty in politics, where things are not always as they seem? Will it be able to withstand the harsh media offensive? This is often a beautiful love story for those who love stories of affection. Hugh and Aggie in love completely and their joke and conversation is a good vision. However, although the film tries to show political life in its reality, it did not quite work. Did not matter. The production values ​​are high and the script is written with great elegance. With these advantages and intermediaries in Keaton and Harris, those who sit down to the film will find it a good entertainment.
