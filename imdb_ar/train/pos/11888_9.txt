It was very warming. As an expatriate French, I particularly enjoyed this "TV" film, which I think deserves a wider distribution. The representation was good, and the links between each member of this French rural family seemed very strong and very natural. There is no doubt that it is a wonderful film about the concept of family and integration in the face of homosexuality and AIDS. One of the strongest aspects of the film is this distinctive relationship between the eldest son (Leo), who was infected with HIV, and the youngest son (Marcel), who is trying to rest the family hide position. The two people gradually explore each other as time runs out for Leo, who does not want to spend the rest of his life alive under drugs ...
