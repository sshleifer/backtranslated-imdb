In one of Neil Simon's best plays. The mysterious former Fudville stars, played by Walter Matau and George Burns, collide in a private television movie. The problem is they have not seen each other for more than a decade. Filled with graceful linings and one within the Chubbies jokes, this story flies along with a constant stream of humor. He also worked well with Richard Benjamin as my nephew Hardy, Rosita Lenoir as a nurse, and Howard Hessman as a television commercial director. Steve Allen and Felice Diller appear as themselves. Trivial Note: The opening montage features footage of the Hollywood Revo of 1929 featuring Mary Dreisler, Bissey Loew, Pauline Moran, Cliff Edwards, Charles King, Goss Edwards, and Brooks Sisters.
