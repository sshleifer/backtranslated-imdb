The pleasure that was present in the other "films" has practically disappeared with this third effort, which means that imperfect production values ​​are more evident than ever. Another part tinkered ("The Chinese Web"), it suffers too much padding, not to mention the weakness of Spidey's action in places uninspired such as a car park, an apartment and a print shop. Nicholas Hammond is still endearing, fighting valiantly against dull production and the lame performance of the rest of the cast. The plot, in which Peter and Spidey help a Chinese official to put an end to a corruption charge during the Second World War by locating three marines that can testify to his innocence, does he not scream exactly "the book of comics come to life "?
