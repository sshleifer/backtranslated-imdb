Compared to the famous Hollywood remake, this movie is less flashy but much more powerful to give an overall picture of the problem of drugs. The remake loses the most interesting of the three threads of the plot, that of a farmer whose meager livelihood depends on drugs as a lucrative cash crop and whose increased participation in the drug trade for the purpose of to improve destroys herself and her family. In addition, the story of the girl's addiction addresses not only the problems of withdrawal, but also the high risk of relapse. This original mini-series makes good use of the extra time spent studying problems and remains stronger than ever on this controversial topic.
