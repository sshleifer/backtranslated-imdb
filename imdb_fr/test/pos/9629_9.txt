The Patriot is a well-thought-out, well-produced film that will directly draw the viewer into the story and keep him involved to the end. Certainly, it is not in the same vein as Under Siege, and. al., but this is definitely the sci-fi film of what could be described as the second era of Seagal's films. Not so much as action movies with a nod to a story for good measure, but a good story, with one point, and injected with some of Seagal's inimitable action sequences. Is this the kind of movie that hardcore action addicts will enjoy? Probably not. But for those of us who not only love our action, but can also enjoy a well-told story, it's a big step forward for Seagal. It's almost as if the transformation was done by Clint Eastwood as he tried to get out of the shadows of his "Dirty Harry" series. One or the other of the men, if he had continued in the singular direction they had chosen, would have always been assigned to a specific role and would have guaranteed the brevity of their career ...
