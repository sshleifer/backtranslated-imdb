Angela (Sandra Bullock) is a computer scientist but, being shy and a little recluse, she does all her work in the confines of her apartment. As she prepares to take a vacation to Mexico, a colleague sends her a computer disk containing disturbing information. Angela agrees to meet her colleague, but he mysteriously dies in a plane crash. Angela goes to Mexico but takes the record with her. As she lounges on the beach, a remarkable looking gentleman, Jack (Jeremy Northam) makes him openings. She falls in love with them and the two end up on a boat for Cozumel. However, Jack works for the people who have generated the secret information on the disk and he is ready to get it. Even after Angela has escaped from his clutches and lands in the United States, Jack makes things difficult. It changes Angela's identity on every computer in the country, causing her to lose her apartment, her bank account, and so on. Can Angela, an IT expert, beat Jack at his own game? This very exciting film has many assets. In the first place, Bullock and Northam are two very beautiful and interesting actors, and their presence adds an immediate appeal. The script is very intelligent and secure in its knowledge of the capabilities of computers and their relevance in today's world. The costumes, the sets, the production and the making of the film are also very interesting. And, despite appearances, there is a lot of thrilling action as Angela races to defeat her enemy. If you like thrillers without bloodshed or violence, this is an excellent choice. It offers twists with great frequency, allowing the viewer to "net" a very good evening of entertainment.
