What attracts a man to military service? What prepares a man to survive brutal torture as a prisoner of war? What despair leads to the planning and execution of an escape in the jungles of Southeast Asia? How does he deal with the ghosts and memories he comes home with? Herzog tries to answer these questions in his documentary about Dieter Dengler, a German emigrant and a US Navy pilot shot dead in Laos in 1966, prisoner, tortured and hungry, but who eventually escapes to be haunted by this experience. . A powerful and personal account of the man, his life and his experience, recounted through the seamless integration of interviews, archive footage and new sequences. You will never forget this story - or this man.
