Skenbart is one of the funniest films ever created by Peter Dalle, but also by the Swedish film industry. It's a film made in black and white to get something of the atmosphere of the days before Christmas in December 1945, which it does very well. Almost all the plot takes place on a train, non stop to Berlin. On the train are a mix of homosexuals, nuns, deported refugees, murderers, alcoholics and the failed literary critic "Gunnar" played by the famous Swedish actor Gustav Hammarsten. The main role 'Gunnar' is the kind of person who, although his intentions are for the best, seems to lead everyone around him, in an extremely funny way, in a catastrophe and in a real hell, especially for the Finnish war. Return to the country, wounded soldier played by the extremely funny comedian Robert Gustafsson. In the train is also a doctor who cheats on his wife with his mistress. Together, they planned to murder the doctor's wife who is also traveling on the same train without having any idea of ​​her husband's intentions. Will the doctor's wife escape his plan to murder him and will all the others survive the unfortunate "Gunnar"?
