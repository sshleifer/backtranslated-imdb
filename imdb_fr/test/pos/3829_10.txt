Without doubt one of the best episodes of all time, Balance of Terror contains 45 minutes of well executed suspense, with intelligent parallels in the real world (the title refers to a situation very similar to that prevailing between the States United States and the Soviet Union during the Cold War.), The characteristics and introduction of the second most important hostile extraterrestrial race of Star Trek after the Klingons: the Romulans. <br /> <br /> After receiving a distress call from an outpost of the Federation, the Enterprise is dangerously close to the Neutral Zone, which, if crossed, would result in an open conflict with the Romulans, although no one has ever seen her in the flesh. Soon, a Romulan ship appears, equipped with a new weapon and a camouflage device that makes it almost impossible to defeat. Faced with the threat of imminent annihilation, Kirk must engage in a battle of wit with the Romulan commander (Mark Lenard) to ensure the survival of his crew. Unfortunately, the task is made more difficult when one of the men accuses Spock of being in alliance with the enemy, because of the physical resemblance that exists between Romulans and Vulcans, two races that are actually related in a distant way (a fact quite ironic). in retrospect, since Lenard was playing Spock's father, Sarek, starting in season 2.) <br /> <br /> Always very critical of war, Star Trek enjoys one of his best moments with the most striking and tense take the subject. Although the Romulans are not really based on the Soviets (the name is actually borrowed from Romulus, the founder of Rome), the scenario is obviously inspired by the very strong fear that the American and Russian citizens felt at the time from both countries. capable of destroying the other with nuclear weapons (this fear gave birth to the concept of the "balance of terror"). But even without the subtext, it remains an essential episode, due in large part to the intellectual battle between the two adversaries, which results in a fascinating duel between Shatner and Lenard. No need to say who wins ...
