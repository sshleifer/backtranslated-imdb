Well-done drama based on an Arthur Miller novel. Something as simple as a pair of glasses becomes a shattering. Lawrence Newman (William H. Macy) is a man who has chosen to be satisfied with his mundane life. the same job for twenty years and still living with his mother. His boss tells him to correct his vision with a pair of glasses. Newman's life changes dramatically and plunges him into hell. The glasses he chose give him a Jewish air. He lost his job and became the subject of a close examination by his Brooklyn neighborhood. In search of a job, he meets the seductive and frank Gertrude (Laura Dern), herself in conflict with her Jewish appearance. Soon, the new life of the couple becomes a nightmare filled with humiliation and attacks dictated by sectarianism. A very fitting cast that features: Joseph Ziegler, Peter Oldring, Kay Hawtrey and Music Fame, Meat Loaf.
