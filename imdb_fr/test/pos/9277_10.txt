"Watch the Skies" (2005 - 60 minutes) is an excellent documentary on science fiction movies. It was produced and directed by critic Richard Schickel, author of more than 20 books on this topic. Mark Hamill is the narrator of the documentary. Schickel teams up with directors Steven Spielberg, James Cameron, George Lucas and Ridley Scott for a trip back in time and space to present some of the most memorable sci-fi movies of the fifties as well as classics plus Recent. The documentary presents six different approaches: The paranoia of atomic warfare; Fantastic journeys on the moon; The enigmatic planet Mars; Good and bad extraterrestrials; The world after apocalyptic; and the future of humanity. It features comments and scenes from the following classics: Flying Saucers, Rocketship XM, Destination Moon, Space Kids, The Beast at 20,000 Fathoms, Godzilla: The King of Monsters, The Incredible Shrinking Man, The Killer attack Tomatoes, A trip to the moon, the angry red planet, the forbidden planet, the thing from another world, the Earth against flying saucers, I married a monster from outer space, the invasion of the bodies Snatchers, the blob, the war of the worlds, the day the Earth stopped, AND: The alien, The Omega Man, The Planet of the Monkeys, The Terminator, 2001: An odyssey of the space, Things to come and metropolis. Highly recommended for fans of Science Fiction!
