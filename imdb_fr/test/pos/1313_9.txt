Mishima: A Life in Four Chapters is an artist biography about famous Japanese writer Yukio Mishima, who resembles Paul Schrader's character, Travis Bickle (taxi driver): both are lonely people seeking their place in their society and when they realize that the world does not need them, they are trying to destroy their surrounding universe. <br /> <br /> If you want to know more about the life of Mishima, you will not find much information here, because it shows that he did not live a very interesting life (except of his last day), but if you want to understand his personality, then it's the best film of its kind, because most of the film is an adaptation of his novels and also provides a guide for his thoughts. This film shows that Mishima was a person who had witnessed the fall of Japanese culture and loved it very much. With his last act, he tried to preserve the traditions and to prove to himself that he was a true warrior. nobody was just a man without power to change events.
