Two years after its initial release, Goldeneye is still at the top of the first-person shooter for the Nintendo 64. Even the Quake and Turok series did not have the combination of graphic detail, sound quality, enemy intelligence , challenge and general fun I come back again and again to this game. The missions have each specific objectives that require you to think as a Bond, not only to film all the bad guys that appear on your screen, but also to avoid cameras, disable security systems, save hostages, protect Bond girl, etc. Q gadgets abound in this game, including the famous watch. The game is loosely based on the script of the film, including all the main characters and the best scenes of the film, from the bungee jump of the dam to the escape of the prop airplane until the chase through Saint -Petersburg. Even the layout is preserved to the extent possible, so you can recognize different situations if you have seen the film. Other levels are added to challenge the player and to chain the scenes a little more. With each level of difficulty, the mission's objectives are more difficult, the enemies smarter and the bullets more deadly. I still have not managed to go through the 00-Agent levels. Cheats can be opened, not by entering codes or pressing buttons, but by completing certain levels in a given amount of time, and additional characters can be opened for multiplayer mode. Multiplayer is always the best among first-person shooters. It's not as crispy as Turok, but it does not slow down as much ... tons of options give your friends the reason to blow up again and again, and again, just for the blows. foot. There are better games for the N64, such as Zelda and Star Wars, but Rare has continued its momentum with outstanding games with a first-person shooter that has not been surpassed and will not be surpassed until in 2000 with Perfect Dark.
