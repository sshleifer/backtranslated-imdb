Helen (Kate Capshaw) owns a bookstore in the sleepy coastal town of Loblolly by the Sea. Divorced, Helen has a girl who will camp during the summer, giving mom a little more freedom. The manager, Janet (Ellen DeGeneres), works in Helen's store. This girl with crazy village gossip who has eyes for the handsome firefighter George (Tom Selleck) and two young students, Jennifer (Julianne Nicholson) and Johnny (Tom Everett Scott). One day, Helen falls on a romantic and tender love letter and she suspects that Johnny wrote it for her. Helen and Johnny start a little "thing". But the letter then falls into the hands of others, including Janet, who thinks George sent it to her, and Johnny, who imagines that Helen wrote it for him. And so on. But, in truth, George may have eyes for Helen and Jennifer has fallen hard for Johnny. The curator of the city museum can also have a secret love. Will tangled things like this ever be settled? It's a cute movie with a beautiful setting and capable stars. Each of the main actors gives a nice performance, including the addition of Blythe Danner and Gloria Stuart to those mentioned above. In addition, the coastal landscape is very beautiful, the costumes very well chosen and the photography very pleasant. There are some surprises, including a subtle development of gay conspiracy, that might not please all viewers. But, for those who love romantic comedies, this one should be included in any list of good choices for the genre.
