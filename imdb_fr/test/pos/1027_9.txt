As a previous commentator, I saw it in 1980 and I have never been able to shake the memory of the captivating story, the splendid acting and the dramatic score. It certainly contains some of Sam Waterston's best works. He and the writers describe Oppenheimer not only as an unjust victim hero - what he was - but also as naive, alcoholic and snobbish, a rounded portrait instead of a stereotype.
