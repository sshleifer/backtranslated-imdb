Making the film as dark and visually as possible to cover budget deficiencies is a strategy often used in low-budget horror films, but it takes it too far. It's SO so dimly lit and cloudy (and it's almost entirely at night) that you often end up looking virtually at a black screen (although the bad video transfer may have something to do with it too). Alas, "gloomy" is also the best word to describe the plot of the film. Filmmakers cast various (and not original) horror ideas with no semblance of logic, and halfway they feel they have almost abandoned the effort to make a good horror movie; you know it when you see characters who are supposed to be in danger of death (or sometimes even dead) chatting ....
