"Rambo: First Blood, Part II" by George P. Cosmatos is a pure fulfillment of wishes. The United States clearly did not win the war in Vietnam. They caused unimaginable damage to this country and this film continues the fairy tale of so innocent soldiers. The only bad guys were the leaders of the nation, who made this war happen. Rambo's character is perfect to notice. He is extremely patriotic, lamenting that Americans in the United States have not appreciated and celebrated the achievements of the soldier, but that he is wary of senior officers and politicians. Like all films that defend the war (for example, "We were soldiers"), we also avoid having to justify in a comprehensible way the commitment in South Asia. And in this respect also the reason for every American-American soldier who was there. Instead, Rambo takes revenge on the wounds of a whole nation.
