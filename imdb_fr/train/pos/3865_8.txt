"Stories of the Century" was a half-hour series and premiered in the 54-55 television season. It was also the first Western television series to win an Emmy Award. The veteran American actor Jim Davis as railroad detective Matt Clark, the series pitted Clark and his fellow detectives in the railways (Mary Castle as Frankie Adams for the first half of the season and Kristine Miller in "Jonesy" during the second of the outlaws of various periods ranging from the mid-1860s to the early 1900s. The series was very satisfying, easy to look at and quite realistic, mainly because of the easy-going charm Jim Davis in the main role, he seemed to be a real Western character Another note When Matt Clark arrived in town after a long walk, it really felt like a long horse ride because it was covered with dust <br /> <br /> A very good early western.
