There is no romance or other plot in this movie, it's action and intrigue, making it a kung fu movie for man. <br /> <br /> An old master sends his last disciple Yan Tieh to arrest his five former students whose style represents five poisonous animals: centipede, snake, scorpion, lizard and toad. Despite the word "Venom" in the title, none of these students use venom to kill their opponents. Yan Tieh explained to his teacher that he was no match for the five former students. He had to find one with whom he could form an alliance to defeat the other four. How Yan Tieh and the others meet each other is the plot of the story, with a good kung-fu action spread throughout the story. <br /> <br /> Recognized as a cult classic, this film has already become an annual feature film about kung fu. It is well known that other films refer to the five styles described in this story. <br /> <br /> This is not an artistic masterpiece, with the usual bad dubbing and nerdy play, but the film is one of the best of its kind because it is so focused on all the ingredients of the kung-fu action movie of his day and gives an extra concentrated dose. <br /> <br /> A movie to watch absolutely if you are a movie kung-fan.
