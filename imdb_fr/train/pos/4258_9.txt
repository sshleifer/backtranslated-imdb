This film plays in the 60s and tells the story of an Italian family: Romano, his wife Rosa and their two children, Gigi and Giancarlo, emigrated from Solino in Italy to Duisburg, in the Ruhr area. I like this film, because I think it is quite realistic: it shows the problems that many foreign families encounter when they come to another country: they have to get used to a new culture, a new environment and it can be difficult: I do not know the language .... It's difficult for the family, but they find a solution: they open a restaurant offering typical Italian cuisine, named "Solino", as their hometown. The film also shows different conflicts - Gigi and Giancarlo fall in love with the same girl and although Rosa has to work very hard, Romano refuses to pay money to hire more workers, etc. etc. But stop, I do not want to tell you how it's going.
