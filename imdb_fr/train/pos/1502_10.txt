The late Robert Bloch (author of PSYCHO, for those who did not pay attention) wrote this tale of terror and this is one of the scariest films I have ever seen. (I had to walk MILES just to see a movie, and it was usually dark when I left the theater, seeing a horror movie was always disconcerting, but especially when it was as well executed as this one.) The opportunity to see this one several years ago on videotape (which should always be a last resort), I was surprised how well she resisted. Take the test of terrorism: watch it at night, alone, and tell me it's not scary ...
