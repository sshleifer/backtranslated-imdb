Although some may call it a "Cuban Cinema Paradise", the film is more like a film "How Green Was My Valley," a memory film mourning lost innocence. The film cleverly avoids falling into a political trap of taking sides (pro-Castro? Anti-Castro?), Focusing instead on the human frailty of the characters and the importance of the family. Filled with good play, especially the Mexican actress Diana Bracho, who plays Keitel's wife. A masterpiece, full of references to classic films, from CASABLANCA to CITY LIGHTS from Chaplin. Gael Garcia Bernal plays a small essential role for the dramatic gain of the story. Television director Georg Stanford Brown, in a rare return to play (remember THE ROOKIES?), Plays a homeless tramp who serves as a Greek choir, superbly. , which has nothing to do with the movie.
