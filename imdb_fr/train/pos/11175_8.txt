So often with Stephen King's adaptations, you simply get a collection of characters reciting dialogues from the books. It really captures the heart of the book. Maybe because they do NOT use big chunks of text directly from the book, but it's a bit more an improvement of the events in the story. Much of her success is Miko Hughes as a baby Gage. Dale Midkiff and Denise "Tasha Yar" Crosby really act like her parents. There is a scene where Louis cuddles Gage, and they are very natural together. Fred Gwynne is WONDERFUL. He perfectly nails the accent of Maine without falling into parody, he is wise and warm, as Jud should be. (8 out of 10)
