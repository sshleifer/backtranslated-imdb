A mini-film of delights, a short musical based on three songs from Cole Porter's success on Broadway. The first film credited Bob Hope is a delight! He plays the role of a millionaire American playboy on vacation in Paris. The film begins with him sitting at the table of a street cafe talking to his friends about this breathtaking beauty. Suddenly, he sees her a few meters away. he has gone so far that his friends tease him and suggest that he show him your bank book. But Hope claims he can win in less than 30 days without money! They bet polo ponies on the problem and take all their money and identity cards. Hope follows her and when they are alone throws a proposal, she does not believe he is sincere until he sings for her, "You do something to me" Cole Porter. But she has to leave and is trying to make money as a tour guide so she can continue. But when she sees him showing another girl in town, disillusioned, she wants to drop him. He continues to chase her and catches her and her family on a race track where he puts his meager earnings on the last race in the hope of winning enough to impress. Through a series of events and large synchronized dance numbers, he loses the winning ticket and decides to marry him rich or poor. So he wins the girl, the race and the bet and still sings two songs!
