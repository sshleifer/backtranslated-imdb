I remember hitchhiking in Spain at the age of 25, after taking the elevator of what turned out to be two little Italian crooks on the run. They did a lot outside the law, but on the other side, they wore a little portrait of Jesus in the pocket for their protection ... Just and unfair, good and bad, criminal and correct, here in a new combination , outside the categories I knew. "Les Valseuses" gives me, although film and not in real life, an image close to my own experiences: the intensity of each moment as soon as you leave "everyone" and that you seek the crucial moment, all that which comes to you is another state of mind and life form that is "dangerous" because, as we all know, there are people who are not ready for it and who are ready to persecute you for having "stolen" and so on ... This film speaks of "values", it is a story about "what is right or wrong": morality. It is the resurrection of the individual who fights free against 'false morals' and conformism ... There is a danger, because how far can one go with one's own "freedom" and cross one's own borders morals and those of other people? What to do with people who are willing to hurt you, put you in jail or even shoot you for things you do, like "stealing" gasoline from a multinational oil company for your fifth car. 'opportunity? The Valseuses awaken in me these questions, because morality, in contradiction with the usual "media message", is rather complex ...
