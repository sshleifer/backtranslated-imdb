Russ and Valerie discuss the creation of a family. The couple live in a posh apartment and run an auction that deals with valuable collectibles. At the same time, a dedicated adoption agency owner takes a mini-vacation and leaves the orphanage at the expense of his father (Leslie Nielsen). Father Harry is in the rental business and he had the brilliant idea of ​​"renting" children from the orphanage to couples like Russ and Valerie. Harry, aware of the couple's dilemma, offers a family of brothers and sisters for a ten-day rental period! Brandon, Kyle and Molly move into the apartment with their temporary parents, with funny consequences, as the new caretakers lack experience with the kids. But, where is the possibility of a happy ending? It's a beloved family movie. The actors, including Nielsen as a wheeler dealer and Christopher Lloyd as a friendly doorman, are all wonderful. The script is lively and fun and the overall production values ​​are quite high. Yes, if only life could be like that! Orphaned children around the world deserve to be able to prove that they are kind and can give joy to parents who are considering adoption. If you want to show your family a film that is based on good values ​​but is also very entertaining, find this movie. It is guaranteed that everyone laughs, even if their heart melts.
