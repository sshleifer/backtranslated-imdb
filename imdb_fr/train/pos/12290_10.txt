The Hotel Du Nord is a gripping tragedy of guilt in which Marcel Carne has portrayed an amusing story of unhappy love that also functions as a revolt against the cruel world. The film is entirely based on a couple of unlucky lovers. when they thought that suicide would put an end to their misery. The Du Nord Hotel has its own inimitable charm, because its inhabitants have become an essential element of the establishment. There is an element of conviviality since everyone rushes to the Hotel du Nord to eat, chat etc.Marcel Carne remained faithful to the spirit of the films produced in the thirties and forties, the Hotel Du Nord having a certain nostalgic sense. Carne, while recreating the life of the Parisian roads, was able to create a kind of nostalgia for black and white giving to his work a unique genre of poetic realism. The Du Nord hotel can be qualified as quintessence of the cinematographic populism. July 14 ball on the banks of the Canal Saint-Martin remains a magnificent sequence. The film The popularity of the Hotel Du Nord has been declared a national monument.
