The King of Masks is a wonderfully told story that sets family preference for men against human preference for love and companionship. Settled in China in the 1930s, at this time of flood, we meet Wang, an elderly street artist with magical talents and captivating the amazed eyes of all his witnesses. When a famous opera performer sees and befriends Wang, he invites her to join his troupe. However, we learn that Wang's family tradition only allows him to pass on his secrets to a son. Learning that Wang is childless, Wang is encouraged to find an heir before the magic is lost forever. Taking the advice to heart, Wang bought an 8-year-old to fulfill his legacy. he would teach his new son, Doggie, the ancestral art of silk masks. Soon, Wang discovers a fact about Doggie that threatens the rare and dying art. <br /> <br /> Together, Wang and Doggie create a bond and experience the range of emotions that invariably accompany it. The story is absorbing. The setting is serene and the costuming simple. In short, it is an award-winning art film at an international price that can not help but move and inspire.
