For Muslim women in West Africa, married life at the hands of abusive husbands can be very difficult. The community may not explicitly endorse such behavior, but in the same way, it may not yet be ready to consider it a criminal, an attitude that of course allows it to continue. Fortunately, the letter of Cameroonian law promises equality to all and this documentary follows the real exploits of various practitioners of the Cameroonian justice system who try to do justice to a number of women and children. What is remarkable (apart from the uplifting central history) is how, despite their informal nature, the courts are really progressive from a pragmatic point of view, if a case is actually purchased.
