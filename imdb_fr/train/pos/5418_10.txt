Cary Grant, Douglas Fairbanks Jr. and Victor McLaglen are three nineteenth-century Indian soldiers who, with the help of a water boy (Sam Jaffe), rid the area of ​​the deadly cult of thugs. The chemistry between the actors helps to make this film one of the most entertaining films of all time. Sam Jaffe is exceptional as an excluded water boy, who is abused by all and still wants to be accepted as a soldier in the company. Freely inspired by Rudyard Kipling's poem. To see absolutely by all those who like this type of film.
