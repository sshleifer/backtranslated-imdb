Hugh (Ed Harris) is a prominent senator determined to run for president. One day, however, he falls on a former high school classmate named Aggie. Aggie (Diane Keaton) is an accomplished and award-winning author with a charming face and an independent spirit. Hugh is in love. He convinces Aggie to become his fiancé. But will Aggie sacrifice her principles of honesty in the political world, where things are not always what they seem to be? And will it be able to withstand the rigors of a hard media blitz? It is especially a beautiful romance for those who love stories of affection. Hugh and Aggie are absolutely in love and their jokes and conversations are a good point of view. However, although the film tries to show political life in its reality, it does not succeed completely. It does not matter. The production values ​​are high and the script is written with great elegance. With these advantages and the beautiful characters of Keaton and Harris, those who will sit in front of the film will find that it is a good entertainment.
