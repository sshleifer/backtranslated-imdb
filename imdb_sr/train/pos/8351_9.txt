I recall that for 25 years I went for a walk to Spain, drove away from what turned out to be two runaways from Italian little thieves. They did much beyond the law, but on the other hand carrying the portrait of Jesus in their pocket for their protection ... True and unjust, good and bad, criminal and accurate where in a new combination, outside the category I knew. "Les Valseuses" gives me, even though a movie, not a real life, an image close to my own experiences: the intensity of each moment as soon as you leave "everything behind" and you start to matter, whatever comes to your mind, the other is the state of mind and 'dangerous 'form of life, because, as we all know, there are people who are not ready for it and willing to persecute you for' theft 'and so on ... This film touches' values', that's the story of "what is right and what what is not ": moral. It is the resurrection of an individual who fights against "false morals" and conformism ... There is a danger all the time, for how far you can go with your "freedom" and cross your moral boundaries and other people? What to do with people who are willing to hurt you, put you in prison, or even shoot at you for things you do, such as "stealing" petrol from a multinational oil company for you fifth car? Les Valseuses are once again arousing these questions in me, because morality, in contrast to the usual 'media message', is quite complicated ...
