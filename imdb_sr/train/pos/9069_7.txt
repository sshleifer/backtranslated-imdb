If you love films about school bullying, courageous children, funny kids and feeding worms, then you will like to eat fried worms. On the first day of the new school, he discovered that some of his comrades played a joke on him putting worms at his lunch. School silence, Joe and his "team" of friends begin to tease Billy and call him a "boyfriend of worms." Billy decides to play together saying "he constantly eats worms". Joe and his friends do not trust him, but Billy persuades them and bet that Joe can eat ten worms in one day, otherwise he will come to school with worms in his pants. The guys take Billy up. on his bet, leaving a poor child with the task of gaining respect from his colleagues by eating worms, cooked, fried or alive. The film can sound rough, but there are many messages in it. First, it shows true friendship and how to accept people for what they are. It also shows you why some abusers resort to maltreatment of other children. The main character of the movie, Billy, is a powerful and brave person with whom we can all relate. It's easy to sympathize with him while we quietly cheer for him to reach his goal, although we can not always agree with what he is doing or with the choices he makes. The kids in the movie are shown exactly how children are in real life and the movie deserves a lot of credit for it. Children's actors are the stars of this performance, showing true emotions and feelings than most other children's movies show. Some adults may not enjoy this movie, but children will, maybe even teenagers. At this moment, there are hardly any other good movies, so if you're not in a position to see snakes on the plane, try worms on a plate in How to Eat Fried Worms. It's a good-looking movie, not just a Fear Factor for kids.
