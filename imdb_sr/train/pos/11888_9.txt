It was very warm warming. As a French evangelist, I particularly enjoyed this "TV" film, which I think deserves wider distribution. The acting was good, and the links between each member of this rural French family looked so powerful and natural. There is no doubt that this is an excellent film about the concept of family and involvement in the face of homosexuality and AIDS. One of the strongest aspects of the film is this privileged relationship between the oldest son (Leo), who is infected with HIV, and the youngest son (Marcel), to whom the rest of the family is trying to hide the situation. Two characters Progressively explore each other as time runs out for Leah, who is unwilling to spend the rest of his life surviving under the drugs ...
